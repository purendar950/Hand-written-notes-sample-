Railway PYQ Q1. Who is known as the Father of the Indian Constitution? / भारतीय संविधान के जनक के रूप में किसे जाना जाता है?

A
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar ✔
B
Rajendra Prasad
C
Sachchidanand Sinha
D
Jawaharlal Nehru
✅ Answer: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar — "Father of the Indian Constitution" | Also called: Modern Manu / Modern Man
Why Ambedkar = Father? — Drafting Committee
  • Ambedkar was Chairman of the Drafting Committee — most important committee
  • Drafting Committee had 7 members total
  • Chief Draftsman of Constituent Assembly = S.N. Mukherjee (NOT Ambedkar)
  • Formed on: 29 August 1947 | First meeting: 30 August 1947
  • Drafting Committee sat for total: 141 days
7 Members of Drafting Committee
#MemberNote

2Alladi Krishnaswamy Iyer—
3N. Gopalaswami IyengarRemember: Iyer + Iyengar (two names)
4Mohammad Sadullah—
5K.M. Munshi—
6B.L. Mitter → replaced by N. Madhav RaoResigned due to illness
7D.P. Khaitan → replaced by T.T. KrishnamachariDied

Chief Draftsman ≠ Chairman! Chief Draftsman of Constituent Assembly was S.N. Mukherjee. Ambedkar was Chairman of Drafting Committee. बहुत बड़ा EXAM TRAP है यह! 🚨

Three Drafts Submitted
21 Feb 1948 1st Draft published — public given 8 months to discuss & suggest changes
21 Oct 1948 2nd Draft submitted
4 Nov 1948 3rd / Final Draft presented → First Reading began same day

⑤ संविधान सभा की संरचना — 299 Members 🔢
Railway PYQ Q5. The Constitution was drafted by how many representatives? / संविधान का मसौदा कितने प्रतिनिधियों द्वारा तैयार किया गया?
A
299 ✔
B
222
C
159
D
389
✅ Answer: 299 — Originally 389 seats, reduced after Partition + Muslim League boycott
Structure of Constituent Assembly

Original CA (1946):

389 total = 296 from British India + 93 from Princely States

After Partition:

389 → 299 seats remaining (Muslim League boycott + partition)

How 296 chosen: 292 from 11 Governor's Provinces + 4 from Chief Commissioner's Provinces

Princely States: Nomination by kings

Election Method from British India: Indirect Election (not universal franchise)

Provincial Legislative Assemblies elected members → Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote

1 seat per 10 lakh (1 million) people

Same system used today for: Rajya Sabha + Presidential elections

⑰ संघवाद — Federalism & Scholars' Views 🗺️
Railway PYQ Q17. Which of the following is INCORRECT regarding the UNITARY features of the Constitution?
A
All India Services (IAS, IPS, IFS)
B
Appointment of Governor by Centre
C
Single Citizenship
D
Rigid part of the Constitution ✔
✅ Answer: D — Rigid part of Constitution is a FEDERAL feature (not unitary). States need to be consulted for rigid amendments.
UNITARY FEATURES (Centre has more power)
  • All India Services (IAS, IPS, IFS)
  • Flexible part of Constitution
  • Appointment of Governor by Centre
  • Single Citizenship
  • Single Constitution
  • Emergency Provisions
  • Parliament can create/destroy states
FEDERAL FEATURES (States have power)
  • Rigid part of Constitution
  • Bicameralism (Lok Sabha + Rajya Sabha)
  • Dual Government (Centre + State)
  • Independent Judiciary
  • Division of Powers (3 lists)

ScholarCoined Term

⑱ संसदीय बनाम राष्ट्रपति शासन — Parliamentary vs Presidential 🏛️
Railway PYQ Q18. Which of the following statements about the Indian political system is INCORRECT?
A
India is a secular state
B
India has a presidential system of government ✔ (INCORRECT STATEMENT)
C
India has a parliamentary system
D
India has a quasi-federal system
✅ Answer: B — India has PARLIAMENTARY system (NOT presidential). Called: Westminster Model of Government.
Parliamentary vs Presidential — Key Differences
FeatureParliamentary (India/UK)Presidential (USA)
AccountabilityCouncil of Ministers collectively responsible to Lok Sabha (Art 75(3))No accountability to legislature
MinistersMust be MPs (Art 75(5)) or become one within 6 monthsNeed not be legislators
Executive HeadDual: PM (real/de facto) + President (nominal/titular)President = real executive
Separation of PowersNot strict (judicial activism possible)Strict separation / watertight compartments
WESTMINSTER MODEL
India borrowed parliamentary system from UK (Britain)
Called Westminster Model after Westminster, London (where UK Parliament is)
Art 142 = Judicial Activism example (judiciary acting as executive)
