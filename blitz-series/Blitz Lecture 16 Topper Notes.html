⑤ Physics — Electromagnetic Induction & Magnetism ⚡
Q6. A conductor rod of length L moves with velocity V perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field B. What is the induced EMF across it?
A
V/BL
B
BVL ✔
C
B/VL
D
BV/L
✅ Answer: B — EMI (Electromagnetic Induction), discovered by Michael Faraday in 1831. Induced EMF = B×V×L, found using Fleming's Right-Hand Rule

Fleming's Rules — Don't Confuse!

  • Right-Hand Rule — finds INDUCED EMF/current (Electromagnetic Induction)
  • Left-Hand Rule — finds FORCE on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field
🧠 Fleming's Left-Hand Rule — "Father, Mother, Child": Thumb = Force (Father), First finger = Magnetic field (Mother), Middle finger = Current (Child)
Q7. What is the force exerted on a charged particle by combined electric and magnetic fields called?
A
Lorentz Force ✔
✅ Answer: Lorentz Force (not to be confused with the "Lorenz curve" studied in Economics — similar-sounding names!)

Solenoid — Key Facts:

  • Magnetic field lines never intersect; they form closed loops
  • Inside a solenoid: field is uniform and maximum
  • Outside: field is circular, decreasing with distance
  • Inserting a soft iron rod inside a solenoid turns it into an Electromagnet

⑦ Geography — Ghaghra River System & Ganga Tributaries 🌊
Q9. Consider the statements about the Ghaghra River: (1) Originates from the Mapcha Chungo Glacier in the Himalayas; (2) Forms a deep gorge at Shishapani before entering the plains; (3) Merges with the Ganga at Allahabad after receiving the Sharda River.
A
Only 1 and 2 are correct ✔
B
All three correct
C
Only 1 correct
D
Only 2 and 3
✅ Answer: A — Statements 1 & 2 are correct. Statement 3 is WRONG — Ghaghra merges with the Ganga at Chapra, Bihar (NOT Allahabad)

Ganga River System — Tributaries:

  • Left Bank: Ramganga, Gomti, Ghaghra, Gandak, Kosi, Mahananda
  • Right Bank: Yamuna, Son, Punpun
  • Distributary (not tributary): Hooghly (Bhagirathi-Hooghly) — its tributary is Damodar (flows through a rift valley in the Chota Nagpur Plateau, into the Bay of Bengal)
🧠 Gorge vs Canyon: A Gorge has steep cliffs formed by erosional action of running water (e.g., Shishapani on Ghaghra). A Canyon has stepped walls. Both are erosional landforms.

⑮ Polity — Union List (Seventh Schedule) 📜
Q17. Which of the following statements based on Union List entries is/are correct? (1) Parliament has exclusive power to legislate on lotteries by Central/State govts; (2) Cooperative societies incorporation/regulation are in the Union List; (3) RBI, foreign exchange, currency are under Union's legislative domain; (4) Parliament can make laws on corporations not limited to one state (excluding universities).
A
1, 3, 4 are correct (2 is wrong — cooperative societies are a STATE subject) ✔
✅ Answer: Statements 1, 3, 4 are correct. Statement 2 is WRONG — cooperative societies fall under the STATE List, not Union List

Seventh Schedule — Lists:

  • Union List — only Parliament legislates (e.g., lotteries by Centre/State, RBI, foreign exchange, currency, inter-state corporations)
  • State List — only State Legislature (e.g., cooperative societies)
  • Concurrent List — both can legislate; if conflict, Parliament's law prevails UNLESS the State law was reserved for President's assent under Article 200 ("Doctrine of Precedence")

🧠 Exam Strategy Tip: When eliminating options, only remove a choice if you're CONFIDENT about the fact. If unsure, don't eliminate — guessing wrongly by false elimination is worse than leaving the option open.

⑰ Economics — Banking, OMO & Bank Rate 🏦
Q19. Which statement is correct? (1) A bank's main liability is the deposits it holds; (2) Open market operations involve buying/selling government bonds; (3) Bank rate is the rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks.
A
All three are correct ✔
✅ Answer: All three statements are correct

Bank Liabilities vs Assets:

  • Liabilities = Deposits (demand deposits + time/term deposits)
  • Assets = Loans given by the bank
  • Difference between interest earned (on loans) and interest paid (on deposits) = Spread (bank's profit)
Q20. Does the Bank Rate require collateral?
A
No, it does NOT require collateral — that's why Bank Rate is HIGHER than Repo Rate ✔
✅ Answer: No collateral required for Bank Rate lending — hence it carries a HIGHER interest rate than the Repo Rate (which DOES require collateral, e.g., government securities)

Open Market Operations (OMO) — 2 Types:

  • Outright Purchase — selling/buying govt securities. SELL during inflation (absorb liquidity); BUY during recession (inject liquidity, generate demand)
  • Repo — short-term, collateral-based borrowing

Stagflation = Stagnant economy + Inflation + High unemployment (violates the Phillips Curve, which assumes inflation & unemployment move inversely)

㉒ Geography — Insolation & Solar Radiation ☀️
Q26. Which of the following is NOT a primary factor affecting the variation of insolation on Earth?
A
Rotation of the Earth
B
Transparency of the atmosphere
C
Angle of inclination of the sun's rays
D
(whichever factor is unrelated to insolation, per the given options) ✔
✅ Answer: Rotation, atmospheric transparency, and angle of sun's rays ARE genuine factors affecting insolation; presence of water bodies also matters — the answer excludes an unrelated distractor option

Insolation — Key Facts:

  • Insolation = Incoming Solar Radiation (short wavelength). Terrestrial Radiation = outgoing (long wavelength) — trapped by greenhouse gases
  • Greenhouse gases (by contribution): Water vapour > CO₂ > Methane > N₂O
  • Maximum insolation is NOT at the Equator — it's slightly above/below it (Equator has high cloud cover/rainfall, reducing atmospheric transparency)
  • Beyond Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N) / Tropic of Capricorn (23.5°S), direct/vertical rays never fall — only slanting rays (cover more area, less heating per unit area)
  • Arctic/Antarctic Circle (66.5°N/S) — day/night can last up to 6 months

㉓ Biology — Human Reproduction & Gestation 👶
Q27. Which sequence correctly represents the chronological order of human development during gestation?
A
Zygote → Embryo → Fetus ✔
B
Embryo → Zygote → Fetus
C
Fetus → Zygote → Embryo
D
—
✅ Answer: A — Zygote → Embryo → Fetus

Human Reproduction — Process Flow:

  • Sperm enters through the cervix (HPV — Human Papilloma Virus — causes cervical cancer)
  • Egg (largest human cell) released from the ovary; Sperm = smallest human cell
  • Fertilization occurs in the Fallopian tube → forms the Zygote
  • Zygote implants in the thickened lining of the Uterus → develops into Embryo → then Fetus
🧠 Uterus vs Ureter — Don't Confuse! Ureters = tubes from kidneys to the urinary bladder (excretory system). Uterus = female reproductive organ where the embryo develops. Completely different systems!

Natya Shastra — 4 Instrument Categories (Recap):

  • Ghan Vadya — Solid instruments

  • Avanaddha Vadya — Percussion with stretched membrane (e.g., Mridangam, Dholak) — produces sound when struck/shaken/rubbed

㉕ Polity — POCSO Act 👧

POCSO Act (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences), 2012:

  • Deals with sexual offences against children below 18 years of age
  • Gender-neutral — applies regardless of the victim's gender
  • The abettor/instigator receives the same punishment as the offender
  • Minimum punishment: 7 years imprisonment
  • Victim's identity is kept confidential
  • Trials are conducted through Special (Fast-Track) Courts

💡 Topper Tips — Blitz Lecture 16 Ke Liye Yaad Rakho!
  • Vacuoles are LARGER in plant cells (50-90% of volume), NOT animal cells — a frequently reversed trap
  • 10th Schedule = Anti-Defection; 8th = Languages; 9th = Land Reforms; 11th = Panchayats; 12th = Municipalities — don't mix these up!
  • Fleming's Right-Hand Rule = Induced EMF; Fleming's Left-Hand Rule = Force on conductor
  • NCST/NCSC/NCBC are constitutional bodies (Art 338/338A/338B); NHRC/NCW are statutory
  • Ghaghra meets Ganga at Chapra, Bihar — NOT Allahabad (common trap)
  • Max insolation is NOT at the Equator — it's just above/below it, due to cloud cover reducing atmospheric transparency at the Equator itself
  • Cooperative societies = State List subject (frequently mistaken for Union List)
  • Extreme absolute words ("no impact," "does not release," "never," "completely") in Assertion-Reason statements are usually FALSE
