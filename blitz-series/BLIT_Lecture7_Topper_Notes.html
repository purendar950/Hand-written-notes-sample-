① Physics — Kinetic Energy ⚡
Q1. Speed increased to 1.5 times → KE becomes?
A
1.5 times
B
2 times
C
2.25 times ✔
D
3 times
✅ Answer: C — 2.25 times (= 9/4 × original KE)

KE = ½mv²

New v = 1.5v = 3/2 × v

New KE = ½m × (3/2 v)² = ½m × 9/4 × v² = 9/4 × original KE = 2.25 times

Energy Types — Quick Table
Energy TypeDefinitionExample
KineticEnergy due to motionMoving bullet, flowing water
PotentialEnergy due to positionWater in dam, stretched bow
MechanicalKE + PEAny moving body
ENERGY CONVERSIONS
Mechanical → Electrical: Generator/Dynamo
Electrical → Mechanical: Motor
FREE FALL ENERGY
Top: PE = max, KE = 0
Mid: PE = KE (equal)
Bottom: KE = max, PE = 0
KE IN TERMS OF MOMENTUM
KE = p²/2m   (where p = linear momentum)

② Physics — Sound Waves 🔊
Q2. Compression = high pressure, Rarefaction = low pressure. Sound = movement of variations in _____ within medium.
A
Intensity
B
Density ✔
C
Temperature
D
Pressure
✅ Answer: B — Density

Sound waves are Longitudinal waves — particles vibrate back & forth in direction of wave propagation.

TermMeaningOn Graph
CompressionHigh pressure, High particle densityCrest
RarefactionLow pressure, Low particle densityTrough
Sound Wave Properties
  • Cannot travel in vacuum
  • Cannot be polarized (only transverse waves can be)
  • Speed: Highest in Solids → Liquids → Gases
  • Reflection of sound = Echo
  • Repeated reflection of sound = Reverberation
P-waves = Longitudinal = like sound (Compression & Rarefaction)
S-waves = Transverse = like light waves

⑨ Economy — Law of Demand 📈
Q9. When price increases, quantity demanded?
A
Increases
B
Decreases ✔
C
Remains same
D
First increases then decreases
✅ Answer: B — Price ↑ → Demand ↓ (Inverse relationship)
EXCEPTIONS TO LAW OF DEMAND
Veblen Goods: Luxury/status — demand ↑ with price
Giffen Goods: Inferior necessity — demand ↑ with price
LAW OF SUPPLY
Price ↑ → Supply ↑ (Direct relationship)
Equilibrium = where demand and supply curves meet
Important Economic Curves
CurveShapeWhat it shows
Phillips CurveDownward slopingInverse relation: inflation ↔ unemployment
Laffer CurveBell-shapedTax rate vs tax revenue — rises till a point then falls
Lorenz CurveBowed curveIncome inequality — 45° line = perfect equality | More bow = more inequality
GINI COEFFICIENT
0 = Perfect equality | 1 = Perfect inequality | Calculated from Lorenz Curve
