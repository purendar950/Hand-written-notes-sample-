FACE VALUE vs INTRINSIC VALUE
Face value = value printed on the currency · Intrinsic value = value of the material it's made of.
For circulation: face value must be > intrinsic value.
🧠 Mohammad bin Tughlaq's failed token currency: intrinsic value became greater than face value.

Q12. Which of the following is NOT a way to finance a budgetary deficit?
A
Borrowing
B
Printing currency
C
Neither 1 nor 2 (both ARE ways) ✔
D
—
✅ Answer: C — Both borrowing & printing currency are valid ways, so "neither" is correct. Ways = raise taxes, borrow (external/internal), print money.
SEIGNIORAGE
The profit the government earns by printing currency. E.g., print ₹1 crore; printing/paper/staff cost ≈ ₹1 crore... if cost is much lower, the difference is seigniorage. Coins & ₹1 notes = Govt of India; other notes = RBI.

✅ Answer: A — The Gulf War & soaring oil prices triggered the 1991 BOP crisis (only ~2 weeks of forex left) → led to LPG reforms

Q77. In the context of money supply in India, which of the following is NOT a quantitative tool of the RBI?
A
Repo rate
B
Open Market Operations
C
Marginal Standing Facility
D
Moral suasion / Margin requirements ✔
✅ Answer: D — Moral suasion & margin requirements are QUALITATIVE. Quantitative = bank rate, repo, reverse repo, CRR, SLR, OMO, MSF.

Open Market Operations (OMO / sterilization) = buying/selling govt securities. Sell during inflation (suck out money); buy during deflation/recession (inject money). Two types: outright purchase & repo (repurchase obligation).
