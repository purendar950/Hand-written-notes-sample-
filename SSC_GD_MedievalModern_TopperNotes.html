B

✅ Answer: A — Tomb of Itmad-ud-Daulah = "Baby Taj" | Built by Nur Jahan | First use of Pietra Dura
Mughal Monuments — Nicknames & Builders
MonumentBuilt ByLocationNickname / Key Fact

Q4. Who mentioned the role of Banjaras in transporting grain in his memoirs?
A
Jahangir ✔
B
Babur
C
Sher Shah Suri
D
Humayun
✅ Answer: A — Jahangir (ruled 1605-1627) mentioned Banjaras transporting grain to mandis
ALAUDDIN KHILJI'S 3 MARKETS
1. Mandi = Grain Market
2. Sarai Adil = Cloth Market
3. Third Market = Slaves & Horses
Officers: Shahna (Market Controller) + Barids (Spies) + Munhiyans (Secret agents)
Banjaras transported grain to these mandis
Mughal Rulers — Timeline
1526–30 Babur — Founder; Battle of Panipat 1526; introduced gunpowder warfare
1530–40 Humayun — Lost to Sher Shah Suri; exiled 15 years
1555–56 Humayun (returned) — Died falling from library stairs
1556–1605 Akbar — Din-i-Ilahi, Sulh-i-Kul, abolished Jizya
1605–27 Jahangir — Mentioned Banjaras; Nur Jahan's influence; patronized painting
1627–58 Shah Jahan — Golden Age of architecture; Taj Mahal, Red Fort
1658–1707 Aurangzeb — Reimposed Jizya; longest Mughal reign

⑤ Medieval History — Cities of Delhi 🏰
Q5. Who built the city of Lalkot in Delhi?
A
Prithviraj Chauhan
B
Alauddin Khilji
C
Humayun
D
Anangpal Tomar ✔
✅ Answer: D — Anangpal Tomar (Tomar Dynasty) built Lalkot — the first city of Delhi
Seven Cities of Delhi — Timeline
1st Lalkot — Built by Anangpal Tomar (Tomar Dynasty); near Saket Metro; completed by Prithviraj Chauhan
2nd Siri — Built by Alauddin Khilji (Khilji Dynasty)
3rd Tughlaqabad — Built by Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq
4th Jahanpanah — Built by Muhammad Bin Tughlaq
5th Firozabad — Built by Firoz Shah Tughlaq
6th Dinpanah — Built by Humayun (later rebuilt by Sher Shah as Shergarh)
7th Shahjahanabad — Built by Shah Jahan (Old Delhi / Red Fort area)

Tomar vs Chauhan Confusion: Anangpal Tomar BUILT Lalkot, but Prithviraj Chauhan COMPLETED it after Chauhans took over from Tomars. Exams often confuse these two! Prithviraj III fought Mohammad Ghori — 1st Battle of Tarain 1191 (Chauhan won), 2nd 1192 (Ghori won).

BATTLES OF TARAIN
1st Battle (1191) — Prithviraj III WON
2nd Battle (1192) — Mohammad Ghori WON
After 1192: Ghori's general Qutbuddin Aibak established Slave Dynasty
DELHI SULTANATE ORDER
1. Slave/Mamluk (1206-1290)
2. Khilji (1290-1320)
3. Tughlaq (1320-1414)
4. Sayyid (1414-1451)
5. Lodi (1451-1526)

⑥ Medieval History — Mughal Revenue System 🏛️
Q6. Why wasn't the Zabt system implemented in Gujarat and Bengal?
A
Lack of officials
B
High rainfall
C
Difficulty in surveying land ✔
D
Opposition from farmers
✅ Answer: C — Surveying land was very difficult there
Zabt/Zabti (Dahsala) System — Key Points
ZABT SYSTEM
Zabt = Zabti = Dahsala System (1580, Akbar's time)
Implemented by: Raja Todar Mal
Method: 10-year average land revenue calculation
NOT applied in Gujarat & Bengal due to difficulty in land surveying
Land Categories under Zabt
CategoryMeaningFallow Period
PolajContinuously cultivatedNever fallow
ParautiTemporarily fallow1–2 years
ChacharMedium fallow3–4 years
BanjarLong-term fallow5+ years

⑧ Medieval History — Akbar's Policies 📜
Q11. Mughal policy of "Sulh-i-Kul" emphasized:
A
Religious tolerance & Universal Peace ✔
B
Aggressive conquest
C
Abolition of taxes
D
Only Islamic law
✅ Answer: A — Introduced by Akbar, meaning "Universal Peace"

Akbar's Religious Policies:

  • Sulh-i-Kul = Universal Peace & Religious Tolerance
  • Din-e-Ilahi = Syncretic religion (combined all faiths) — largely failed
  • Birbal = First & ONLY Hindu to adopt Din-e-Ilahi
  • Ibadat Khana = Hall of Worship at Fatehpur Sikri (religious debates)
  • Abolished Jizya (tax on non-Muslims) & Pilgrimage tax
