Q1. Which mosque in the Agra Fort was constructed by Shah Jahan for the royal ladies and features three domes?

Moti Masjid

Mina Masjid

Q3. Which Mughal-era monument is often called the "Baby Taj" and marks a stylistic shift toward white marble architecture?
A
Tomb of Itmad-ud-Daulah ✔
B
Bibi Ka Maqbara
C
Moti Masjid
D
Humayun's Tomb
✅ Answer: A — Tomb of Itmad-ud-Daulah = "Baby Taj"
  • Built by Nur Jahan (Mehrunissa) for her father
  • First use of Pietra Dura (white marble inlaid with precious stones)
  • Bibi Ka Maqbara = "Taj of Deccan" (NOT Baby Taj), built by Aurangzeb/Prince Azam Shah for wife Rabia-ud-Durani in Aurangabad (now Sambhaji Nagar)
  • Humayun's Tomb = First Char Bagh style garden tomb, architect Mirak Mirza Ghiyas, built by Haji Begum

Q6. Why wasn't the Zabt (Zabti) system implemented in provinces like Gujarat and Bengal under the Mughal Empire?
A
Lack of trained officials
B
High rainfall
C
Difficulty in surveying land ✔
D
Opposition from farmers
✅ Answer: C — Surveying land in Gujarat & Bengal was very difficult
  • Zabti/Zabt = Dahsala system introduced in 1580 by Akbar's finance minister Todar Mal
  • Dahsala = 10-year average-based land revenue
  • Land divided into 4 categories: Polaj (never fallow), Parauti (1–2 years fallow), Chachar (3–4 years fallow), Banjar (5+ years fallow)
Q7. In the Mansabdari system, what term referred to the cavalrymen maintained by the Mansabdar?
A
Zat
B
Sawar ✔
C
Mansab
D
Jagir
✅ Answer: B — Sawar = cavalrymen/horsemen
  • Zat = foot soldiers
  • Mansab = rank
  • Mansabdari ranged from 10 to 10,000
  • Higher mansabs reserved for Sultan's sons
  • Two types of payment: Naqdi (cash, for small mansabs) and Jagirdari (land grants, for large mansabs)
  • System introduced during Akbar's time

Q15. The Iqta system under the Sultanate resembled the feudal system of Medieval Europe mainly because:
A
Revenue rights in exchange for military service ✔
B
Religious control of land
C
Democratic land distribution
D
