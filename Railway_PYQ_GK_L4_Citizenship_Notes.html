② Single Citizenship & Borrowed Provisions 🇬🇧
Q4. The Constitution of India prohibits which of the following types of citizenship?
A
Dual citizenship ✔
B
Single citizenship
C
Acquired citizenship
D
Citizenship by birth
✅ Answer: A — India prohibits dual citizenship → India has single citizenship (borrowed from the UK)

Provisions borrowed from the UK (British Constitution):

  • Single citizenship
  • Parliamentary form of government (Westminster model)
  • Rule of Law (concept by A.V. Dicey)
  • Bicameralism
  • Martial Law
  • First-Past-the-Post system
  • Prerogative writs

③ Commencement of Citizenship Provisions 📅
Q5. When did the citizenship provisions in the Constitution come into effect?
A
15 August 1947
B
26 November 1949 ✔
C
19 July 1949
D
26 January 1950
✅ Answer: B — Citizenship provisions came into effect on 26 November 1949 (the day the Constitution was adopted) — they were not made to wait 2 months.
26 Jan 1930 Purna Swaraj Day (first Independence Day celebrated)
26 Nov 1949 Constitution adopted (Constitution Day) — citizenship, elections & Provisional Parliament provisions came into force immediately
26 Jan 1950 Constitution came into force (date chosen to honour 1930 Purna Swaraj Day)

Articles 5–11 deal with citizenship at the commencement (before the Constitution). Citizenship after commencement is governed by the Citizenship Act, 1955.

A
Article 5 ✔
B
Article 7
C
Article 10
D
Article 11
✅ Answer: A — Art 5 = citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution
ArticleProvision
5Citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution
6Rights of citizenship of persons who migrated to India from Pakistan

WHY ONLY PARLIAMENT? (Art 11)
Citizenship is a subject of the Union List, so only Parliament can make laws regarding it.

⑤ Migration & Cut-off Dates — Articles 6 & 7 🚪
Q7. Which provision addresses the citizenship of people who migrated from Pakistan before the commencement of India's Constitution?
A
Article 5
B
Article 6 ✔
C
Article 8
D
Article 9
✅ Answer: B — Art 6 (rights of citizenship of persons who migrated to India from Pakistan)

Article 6(b)(1) — cut-off date = 19 July 1948:

  • Migrated from Pakistan before 19 July 1948 & ordinarily resident → citizen ✔ ("Happy Birthday!")
  • Migrated after 19 July 1948 → must reside in India ≥ 6 months, then apply & be registered
Q8. Per Article 7, any person who migrated from the territory of India to the territory now in Pakistan after ___ shall not be deemed a citizen of India.
A
1 March 1947 ✔
B
19 July 1948
C
15 August 1947
D
26 January 1950
✅ Answer: A — Art 7 cut-off = 1 March 1947. Those who left for Pakistan after this & later returned need a valid passport/permit to apply for citizenship.
🧠 Two cut-off dates: Article 6 (migrated FROM Pakistan) → 19 July 1948 · Article 7 (migrated TO Pakistan, then returned) → 1 March 1947
