Q9. "Moor's Last Sigh" — acclaimed for blending historical events with magical realism to explore Indian identity and politics — was written by whom? Assess: (A) it won the Booker Prize for innovative storytelling; (R) written by Salman Rushdie.
A
A is FALSE (didn't win Booker), R is TRUE (Salman Rushdie) ✔
✅ Answer: "Moor's Last Sigh" did NOT win the Booker Prize (only Midnight's Children did, for Rushdie). It IS written by Salman Rushdie

Pattern Alert! In CGL statement-based questions, whenever a statement claims a book "won the Booker Prize" — it is USUALLY FALSE unless it's a genuinely famous prize-winner (like Midnight's Children or God of Small Things). Always double-check award claims!

  • Joseph Anton: A Memoir
  • The Golden House
  • Midnight's Children (again — his most famous)

㉘ Exam Strategy — How to Attempt Unknown Book Questions 🎯

Statement-Based Question Tricks (used heavily in CGL):

  • A book's central theme can only be about ONE subject. If two statements describe COMPLETELY different themes (e.g., "urban development" vs "colonial architecture history"), at most ONE can be true — take a 50/50 guess if you can't eliminate further
  • Whenever a statement claims a book "won the Booker/Sahitya Akademi Award" — verify carefully. In this exam's pattern, such claims are OFTEN FALSE unless it's a genuinely famous winner you already know (Midnight's Children, God of Small Things, White Tiger, Heart Lamp, Tomb of Sand)
  • If a statement (Reason) is talking about the SAME general theme as the Assertion (e.g., both about feminist themes in a story collection), and NEITHER mentions awards/prizes, it's likely that both are true and R correctly explains A
  • If two options describe geographically/thematically incompatible settings (e.g., "Sri Lankan civil war" vs "Gandhara region excavations" for a Sri Lankan author's book), use author nationality to eliminate the wrong one

When All Else Fails — SKIP! For extremely obscure books/authors with no elimination possible (e.g., "The Bonkers" by Natasha Sharma), skipping the question is the smartest strategy. Don't waste time overthinking unknowable trivia.

💡 Topper Tips — Books & Authors Ke Liye Yaad Rakho!
  • Kazuo Ishiguro = the "Gothic style" of this topic — his name repeats the MOST across options; memorize ALL his books
  • "An Area of Darkness" = V.S. Naipaul; "An Era of Darkness" = Shashi Tharoor — one-letter difference, different authors!
  • "A Suitable Boy" (Vikram Seth) ≠ "An Unsuitable Boy" (Karan Johar) — don't confuse!
  • "Midnight's Children" (Salman Rushdie) ≠ "Midnight is a Place" (John Aiken) — similar-sounding titles, different books
  • Award-claim statements in CGL are usually FALSE unless the book is a genuinely famous prize-winner you already recognize
  • Satyajit Ray was a film director, NOT a book author — watch for this trap in options!
  • A book's theme = only ONE focus; eliminate multi-theme-claiming options immediately
  • Mother-daughter duo: Anita Desai (Clear Light of Day) & Kiran Desai (Inheritance of Loss) — don't mix up their individual works!
