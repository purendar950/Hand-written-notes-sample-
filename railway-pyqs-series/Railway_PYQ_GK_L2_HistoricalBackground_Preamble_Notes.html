📜 PART A — Historical Background: Important Acts 🏛️
Why did Acts come? — Background समझो
  • East India Company came from Britain to trade, but gradually began to rule
  • Battle of Plassey 1757 → Battle of Buxar 1764 → EIC strengthened its position
  • Employees went to Britain poor and returned rich — British Govt alarmed
  • British Govt passed Acts to regulate (control) the affairs of EIC
  • Centralization began in 1773, peaked in 1833, then Decentralization started in 1861
✦ ✦ ✦
① Regulating Act 1773 — नियामक अधिनियम
  • Governor of Bengal → made Governor General of Bengal (MORE powers than Madras & Bombay)
  • 1st Governor General of Bengal = Warren Hastings (impeachment charges filed against him in Britain)
  • Supreme Court established in Calcutta in 1774 — 1 Chief Justice + 3 Additional Judges
  • 1st Chief Justice of Supreme Court = Elijah Impey
  • Governors of Madras & Bombay had to report to Governor General of Bengal
  • Court of Directors managed commercial affairs of EIC
  • Centralization STARTED from this Act — power concentrated in Bengal

✦ ✦ ✦
② Pitt's India Act 1784 — पिट्स इंडिया एक्ट
  • Board of Control established → to control Civil & Military matters of British India
  • Court of Directors (already existed) = managed commercial affairs
  • Board of Control = managed administrative / political / civil / military affairs
  • This Act created a double government — Board of Control + Court of Directors
  • Board of Control was later abolished by GoI Act 1858

② Charter Acts — 1813 | 1833 | 1853 📃
③ Charter Act 1813
  • ₹1 Lakh allocated for education in India
  • Christian missionaries allowed to come to India
  • EIC's commercial monopoly abolished — EXCEPT: Tea trade & Trade with China
④ Charter Act 1833 — St. Helena Act
  • Abolished remaining exceptions (Tea + China trade) → EIC became purely Administrative body (no more trade!)
  • Governor General of Bengal → Governor General of India (more powers over ALL of India)
  • 1st Governor General of India = William Bentinck
  • Law Commission established → 1st Chairman = Thomas Babington Macaulay
  • Macaulay was also a Law Member in the Governor General's Executive Council
  • Act valid for next 20 years (time limit mentioned)
⑤ Charter Act 1853
  • Legislative & Executive functions of GG's Council formally SEPARATED
  • Introduced Open Competition in Indian Civil Services (ICS)
  • Introduced Local Representation in the Indian Legislature
  • No time limit mentioned → signal to EIC that their rule could end anytime!
  • First time Indians got local representation in Legislature

③ Government of India Act 1858 🏴 — Coin Proclamation
⑥ Government of India Act 1858 — "Act for Good Governance" / "Coin Proclamation"
  • Why? → 1857 Revolution provided excuse to abolish EIC completely
  • Abolished East India Company entirely → Direct British Crown Rule established
  • Position of Secretary of State created + 15-member Council to assist him
  • Governor General → Viceroy of India
  • 1st Viceroy of India = Lord Canning
  • Abolished Board of Control (from Pitt's India Act 1784) and Court of Directors
  • Based on Peel Commission recommendations → number of Indians in British Indian Army reduced

④ Indian Councils Acts — 1861 | 1892 | 1909 🏛️
⑦ Indian Councils Act 1861
  • Decentralization STARTED → Legislative powers restored to Bombay & Madras
  • Three Indians nominated to Legislative Council by Viceroy Canning:
    ① Raja of Banaras   ② Maharaja of Patiala   ③ Sir Dinkar Rao
  • Indians got representation in legislative process for the FIRST TIME
  • Introduced Portfolio System & Ordinance Power (both from this Act)

⑨ Indian Councils Act 1909 — Morley-Minto Reforms
  • Introduced Separate Electorates for MUSLIMS — first communal representation
  • Legislative Council strength increased: 16 → 60 members
  • First Indian in Viceroy's Executive Council (inner cabinet) = Satyendra Prasad Sinha
  • Allowed Supplementary Questions in Legislative Council
  • Named after: Morley (Secretary of State) + Minto (Viceroy)
  • Lord Minto = Father of Communalism in India 🔴
⚠️ EXAM TRAP — 1909 vs 1861
Legislative Council (1861) = Indians in lawmaking = Nominated
Executive Council (1909) = Indians in Cabinet = Satyendra Prasad Sinha
These TWO are different! Don't confuse them.

⑤ Government of India Act 1919 — Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms 🏛️
⑩ Government of India Act 1919 — Montagu-Chelmsford / Montford Reforms
  • Introduced Dyarchy (Dual Government) in PROVINCES (NOT Centre)
  • Provincial subjects divided into: Reserved + Transferred
  • Reserved Subjects (important, Governor NOT answerable): Police, Prison, Land Revenue, Finance
  • Transferred Subjects (less important, Governor answerable): Health, Education, Local Govt, Agriculture
  • Introduced Bicameral Legislature at Centre (Council of State + Legislative Assembly)
  • First introduced Direct Elections in India
  • Provincial Budget separated from Central Budget
  • Separate electorates extended to: Sikhs, Anglo-Indians, Christians (1909 had only Muslims)
  • Public Service Commission established (both Centre & Provinces)
  • Named after: Montagu (Secretary of State) + Chelmsford (Viceroy)

⑥ Government of India Act 1935 — महत्वपूर्ण अधिनियम 🏛️
⑪ Government of India Act 1935 — Most Influential Act
  • Abolished Dyarchy from Provinces → gave Provincial Autonomy
  • Introduced Dyarchy at Centre (reverse of 1919!)
  • Gave Bicameral Legislature to Provinces — but only 6 out of 11 provinces
  • Three Legislative Lists: Federal List + Provincial List + Concurrent List
  • Residual Powers given to the Viceroy (Centre today has them in our Constitution)
  • Established Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
  • Established Federal Court in 1937 (later replaced by Supreme Court of India)
  • Abolished the 15-member Council of the Secretary of State (from 1858 Act)
  • Proposed All India Federation — but NEVER happened (princely states refused)
  • Based on: Principle of Federation & Parliamentary System
  • Approximately ~40% of Indian Constitution came from this Act!

⑦ Indian Independence Act 1947 — स्वतंत्रता अधिनियम 🇮🇳
⑫ Indian Independence Act 1947
  • Based on Mountbatten Plan (Partition Plan) 1947
  • Divided British India into TWO Dominions: India & Pakistan
  • Princely states given choice: join India, join Pakistan, or stay independent
  • Office of Secretary of State ABOLISHED (was created by 1858 Act)
  • Office of Viceroy ABOLISHED
  • British Paramountcy LAPSED — ended British rule completely

📜 PART B — Preamble (उद्देशिका) 🇮🇳
What is a Preamble?

An introductory statement in a constitution that states the reasons and guiding values of the constitution is called the Preamble.

Railway PYQ P1. An introductory statement in a constitution that states the reasons and guiding values of the constitution is called ___. This concept was borrowed from which country's constitution?
A
UK
B
USA ✔
C
Ireland
D
France
✅ Answer: B — USA | Preamble concept borrowed from USA | USA Preamble also starts with "We the People of USA…"

"WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a
SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC
and to secure to all its citizens:
JUSTICE, social, economic and political;
LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;
EQUALITY of status and of opportunity;
and to promote among them all
FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation;
IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949,
do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION."

Key Cases & Persons related to Preamble
Case / PersonYearHeld / Said
Berubari Union Case 1960 Preamble is NOT part of Constitution → cannot be amended
Kesavananda Bharati Case 1973 Preamble IS part of Constitution → CAN be amended (reversed Berubari decision)
42nd Amendment 1976 Added Socialist, Secular, Integrity → Only amendment to Preamble so far
N.A. Palkhiwala — Called Preamble the "Identity Card" of the Constitution
K.M. Munshi — Called Preamble the "Political Horoscope" of the Constitution
Objective Resolution Presented: 13 Dec 1946; Adopted: 22 Jan 1947 Preamble is inspired by Objective Resolution (NOT the same as Preamble!) Presented by Nehru.

EXAM TRAP — Objective Resolution vs Preamble:
Objective Resolution was presented on 13 Dec 1946 and adopted on 22 Jan 1947.
Preamble was adopted along with the Constitution on 26 November 1949.
The Preamble is INSPIRED BY the Objective Resolution — it is NOT the Objective Resolution!
K.M. Munshi headed the Order of Business Committee and was a member of the Drafting Committee.

Preamble — What it IS and what it is NOT:

  • Preamble IS: Guiding Principle for Interpretation; Part of Constitution (after 1973 case); Amended ONCE (42nd, 1976)
  • Preamble is NON-JUSTICIABLE — Courts cannot enforce it directly
  • Preamble is NOT: Source of power for Govt; Limitation on Govt powers; Enforceable in court
  • Preamble shows: Source of the Constitution (People of India); Nature of State; Objectives; Date of Adoption
