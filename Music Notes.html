🗂️ The 10 Thaats (Parent Scales) — by Pandit Bhatkande

Thaat = a family / parent scale. Ragas = children of that family. Most ragas are classified under these 10 thaats defined by Pandit V.N. Bhatkande.

  • The 10 Thaats: Bhairav, Bhairavi, Asavari, Bilawal, Kafi, Kalyan, Khamaj, Marwa, Purvi, Todi
ThaatRagas under it
BilawalAlhaiyya Bilawal, Yamini Bilawal, Shankara
KalyanYaman, Bhupali, Hindol, Kedar, Kamod, Shyam Kalyan, Bihag
KhamajKhamaj, Desh, Tilak Kamod, Rageshwari, Jaijaiwanti, Jhinjhoti
KafiBageshwari, Bhimpalasi, Dhani, Shivranjani, Pilu
BhairavBhairav, Ahir Bhairav, Jogiya, Naat Bhairav, Gauri, Vibhas
MarwaMarwa, Puriya, Bhatiyar, Sahani, Vibhas
PurviPurvi, Shree, Puriya Dhanashree, Basant, Lalit
AsavariAsavari, Jaunpuri, Desi, Darbari Kannada, Adana
BhairaviBhairavi, Malkauns, Vilakshini Todi, Kaunsi Kannada
TodiTodi, Gujari Todi, Madhubanti, Multani
Vilakshini Todi belongs to Bhairavi Thaat — NOT to Todi Thaat. Classic trap!

💡 Key Exam Tips — Yaad Rakho!
  • 4 instrument classes: Tat=string (Chordophone), Avanadh=membrane (Membranophone), Ghan=solid (Idiophone), Sushir=wind (Aerophone)
  • Ragas evolved from Jati; 10 Thaats & notation by Bhatkande; 72-Melakarta by Venkatamakhin
  • Extreme keywords ("only," "exclusively," "without any") in statements → usually wrong
  • Carnatic = South India (Kerala, TN, Karnataka, AP, Telangana); Hindustani = North India
  • Bharat Ratna musicians order: MS Subbulakshmi (1998) → Ravi Shankar (1999) → Bismillah Khan (2001)
  • Teentaal = 16 beats; claps on 1, 5, 13; khaali on 9
