India's Place in the World

  • India occupies about 2.4% of the world's total area
  • India is home to about 17.7% of the world's population
  • India is the 7th largest country in the world by area
  • Australia is the 6th largest country in the world by area

Location — Hemisphere

The Equator divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres, while the Prime Meridian divides it into the Eastern and Western Hemispheres. India lies entirely in the North-Eastern Hemisphere — north of the Equator and east of the Prime Meridian.

Territorial Waters

India's maritime/territorial limit extends up to 12 nautical miles into the sea — roughly equal to 22 km.

🎯 Exam Tip Latitude range (8°–37°) spans about 29°, and longitude range (68°–97°) spans about 29° too — both are used directly to calculate India's Standard Time on the next page!

The Tropic of Cancer & Indian Standard Time

Tropic of Cancer (23½° N)

The Tropic of Cancer passes right through the middle of India, crossing through 8 states, from west to east:

Gujarat→ Rajasthan→ MP→ Chhattisgarh→ Jharkhand→ West Bengal→ Tripura→ Mizoram
🧠 Memory Trick"Gujarati Raja became Chief Justice in the meeting" → Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Tripura, Mizoram.
🎯 Exam Tip Of these 8 states, only 3 have their capital above the Tropic of Cancer: Rajasthan (Jaipur), Tripura (Agartala), and Mizoram (Aizawl). The rest — Gandhinagar, Bhopal, Raipur, Ranchi, Kolkata — lie below it.

Indian Standard Time (IST)

India's longitude spans roughly 30° (68° E to 97° E) — and 30° of longitude equals about a 2-hour time difference. To avoid using two separate time zones, India adopted a single standard meridian at 82°30' E, right in the middle.

Indian Standard Time is 5½ hours ahead of GMT (Greenwich Mean Time / Prime Meridian).

🕐 Fig 2: Tropic of Cancer × 82°30' E — Where They Meet
  • The 82°30' E meridian passes through 5 states: Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Uttar Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Andhra Pradesh (memory trick: "Mauka")
  • It meets the Tropic of Cancer in Chhattisgarh, in the Korea district's Baikunthpur division
❌ Common Mistake Not every country has just one time zone — countries with a large longitudinal spread have many. France has 13 time zones and the US has 6, mainly because of their vast east–west (longitudinal) spread, including overseas territories.

India's International Land Boundaries

India shares a land border with 7 countries, totalling 15,106 km. The list is remembered in decreasing order of border length — Bangladesh has the longest shared border, and Afghanistan the shortest.

🗺️ Fig 3: India's 7 Land Neighbours (longest → shortest)
🧠 Memory TrickChildhood mnemonic: remember Bangladesh, China, Pakistan, Nepal, Myanmar, Bhutan, Afghanistan — 7 countries, already in decreasing order of border length.

Uttar Pradesh's Record

Uttar Pradesh shares its boundary with the most states/UTs of any Indian state — 9 states and 1 Union Territory (Delhi).

🧠 Memory Trick The Radcliffe Line was drawn by Sir Cyril Radcliffe after Partition — it separates India from both Pakistan and Bangladesh, since Bangladesh was originally East Pakistan before becoming independent in 1971.
❌ Common Mistake Don't mix up the 49th Parallel (world's longest international boundary, USA–Canada) with the McMahon Line (India–China) — very different boundaries with very different records!

Northeast & Himalayan Neighbours

States Bordering Bangladesh

Meghalaya, Assam, Mizoram, Tripura, West Bengal — remembered with the trick phrase "Ma'am take water."

States Bordering Myanmar (North → South)

Arunachal Pradesh→ Nagaland→ Manipur→ Mizoram
🧠 Memory Trick"Arunaamami" — Aruna(chal), Naga(land), Mani(pur), Mi(zoram), read top to bottom on the map.

States Bordering Nepal

Sikkim, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, [West] Bengal, Uttarakhand — remembered as "Subu Subu" (S-U-B-B-U).

States Bordering Bhutan

[West] Bengal, Assam, Sikkim, Arunachal [Pradesh] — remembered as "Basa" (B-A-S-A).

🏔️ Fig 4: States Sharing Borders with 3 Countries
🎯 Exam Tip Three states border exactly 3 countries: Sikkim (Nepal, Bhutan, China), Arunachal Pradesh (Bhutan, China, Myanmar), and West Bengal (Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh) — plus the UT of Ladakh (China, Pakistan, and the PoK region bordering Afghanistan).

States Bordering Pakistan

Gujarat, Rajasthan, Punjab, Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh.

❌ Common Mistake Don't forget Ladakh when listing states/UTs bordering Pakistan — it's often left out since it's a Union Territory, not a state, but it does share a Pakistan border.

India's Coastal Boundary

India's coastline length was recently revised by the Government of India, published around late April / early May 2025.

MeasureValue
Old coastline length7,516 km
Revised coastline length (2025)11,098 km
❌ Common Mistake If you learned the old figure of 7,516 km for India's coastline, update it — the Government of India published a revised, more precise figure of 11,098 km in 2025. Don't answer with the outdated number if the question specifies "as per latest data."

Top Coastlines

  • Andaman & Nicobar Islands has the longest coastline overall (including UTs) — both before and after the revision
  • Among states, Gujarat has the longest coastline (approx. 2,300 km) — both before and after the revision
  • Order among states: Gujarat → Tamil Nadu → Andhra Pradesh
  • Goa has the shortest coastline among coastal states
🌊 Fig 5: Coastline Length — Quick Comparison

Coastal States & Union Territories

India has 9 coastal states and 4 coastal Union Territories. Among the UTs:

  • Andaman & Nicobar Islands
  • Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu
  • Puducherry — itself spread across enclaves in three different states: the main Puducherry region and Karaikal (in Tamil Nadu), Mahe (in Kerala), and Yanam (in Andhra Pradesh)
🗺️ Puducherry isn't one single block on the map — it's four separate enclaves (Puducherry, Karaikal, Mahe, Yanam) sitting inside three different states!
🎯 Exam Tip Always double-check whether a coastline question is asking for the old (7,516 km) or revised (11,098 km) figure — recently revised data is a favourite trap in current-affairs-linked geography questions.

  • Area/Population share: 2.4% area, 17.7% population, 7th largest country
  • Extent: N–S 3,214 km, E–W 2,933 km; 8°4'–37°6' N, 68°–97° E
  • Standard meridian: 82°30' E → IST = GMT + 5:30
  • Land neighbours: 7 countries, 15,106 km total
  • Coastline: 11,098 km (revised 2025); Gujarat longest state, Goa shortest, Andaman & Nicobar longest overall
