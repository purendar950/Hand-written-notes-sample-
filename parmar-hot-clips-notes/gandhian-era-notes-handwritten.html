Gandhiji — Early Life & the Journey to South Africa

The period 1915 to 1947 is called the Gandhian Era, because Mahatma Gandhi was the central figure of the entire Indian political scene through this time. But his real political training ground lay years earlier — in South Africa.

💡 Naming ConventionIn Gujarat & Maharashtra, the father's name is placed in the middle — just like Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar (Ramesh = father's name). So: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi!

Gandhiji studied law in England. On returning, he came to Bombay — but could not establish a successful law practice there. Around this time, a client named Abdullah, a local merchant based in South Africa, had a legal case — and Gandhiji travelled to South Africa to handle it, roughly dated around 1898 (though, per the lecture, he had actually visited the region even earlier than this particular case). (Note: "Africa" here always means the country South Africa, not the whole continent.)

👤 Who was Abdullah? (tap to expand)
Abdullah was a local Indian merchant settled in South Africa. His legal case is what first took the young barrister Gandhiji to South Africa — a stay that lasted roughly two decades, during which Gandhiji tested and perfected Satyagraha and the hunger strike, years before bringing these tools back to India.

It was in South Africa that Gandhiji first developed and used Satyagraha and the hunger strike — techniques he later brought to India after 1915.

Racial Discrimination & Three Satyagraha Campaigns

Discrimination against Asians (mainly Indians) in South Africa was rampant. Consider what Indians/Asians actually faced there:

  • Asians were not allowed to go out after 9:00 p.m. — only white South Africans could move about safely at night
  • Asians were confined to specific regions; they could not live anywhere else
  • Asians could not board the first-class compartment on trains

Gandhiji himself experienced this: travelling on the Natal route with a valid first-class ticket, he was still thrown out of the first-class compartment by an officer, simply because he was Asian.

🧠 Fig 2: Discrimination → Three Satyagraha Campaigns

The Three Campaigns (all fought in South Africa)

  1. Satyagraha against Registration Certificates — every Indian/Asian had to carry a registration certificate (like today's Aadhaar card); without it, one could be jailed. Gandhiji led a Satyagraha here and publicly burned such certificates.
  2. Campaign against Indian marriages being declared illegal — the British declared any Indian marriage not conducted according to Christian religious custom as illegal.
  3. Campaign against restriction of Indian migration — Indians were not allowed to freely migrate from one place to another.
Registration Certificates→ Marriages "illegal"→ Migration restricted
💡 Understand it like this...The registration certificate worked just like today's Aadhaar card — compulsory to carry, and if you didn't have one, you could be jailed. Gandhiji's response? Burn the certificates in public, in full view of the authorities!
📖 Gandhiji's Ideological Roots (tap to expand)
Gandhiji was deeply influenced by Leo Tolstoy (hence, Tolstoy Farm) and by John Ruskin — his Satyagraha technique itself was inspired by Ruskin's book "Unto This Last." During these South Africa years, he also wrote Hind Swaraj.
🎯 Exam Tip All three campaigns — Registration Certificates, Marriage Legality, Migration Restriction — happened in South Africa, not India. A favourite exam trap!
❌ Common Mistake Don't assume Satyagraha began in India! Gandhiji tested it first in South Africa — against registration certificates, marriage laws, and migration restrictions — and only brought it to India after 1915.
🧠 Memory Trick Remember "R-M-M" for the three South Africa campaigns: Registration certificates, Marriages (declared illegal), Migration (restricted).

Return to India & the Road to 1917

Gandhiji arrived in India on 9 January 1915 — a date now celebrated as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas (NRI Day). But he did not enter the political scene directly.

🎉 Fun Fact9 January is celebrated every year as Pravasi Bharatiya Divas — precisely because this is the day Gandhiji returned to India from South Africa in 1915!

Gokhale's Advice

Gopal Krishna Gokhale, Gandhiji's political guru, advised him not to get directly involved in any movement or Satyagraha right away. Instead: first travel across India for a year, and learn about the country.

👤 Gopal Krishna Gokhale (tap to expand)
Gokhale founded the Servants of India Society in 1905 to help people. He was also the political mentor of Muhammad Ali Jinnah.
🧠 Fig 3: From Arrival to First Movement

Champaran Satyagraha — Gandhiji's First Satyagraha in India

Between 1917 and 1918, Gandhiji led three important movements back-to-back. The first was the Champaran Satyagraha, in 1917 — his very first Satyagraha in India.

Why Champaran?

Champaran is in Bihar. Farmers here were forced to cultivate indigo on at least 3/20th of their land — a system called Tinkathia ("Tinkathia" literally means 3/20th).

  • By the late 1890s, cheap German synthetic dyes had flooded the market — factory-made, and far cheaper than natural indigo
  • Natural indigo grown by British planters could no longer compete, or cover costs, in the European market
  • So the British forcibly declared that farmers must cultivate indigo on part of their land — the Tinkathia system
📖 Did you know?This wasn't the only indigo-related revolt worth remembering — an earlier Indigo uprising of 1859 had already begun in the Nadia district of West Bengal. And in the Champaran Satyagraha, we see early glimpses of the kind of civil disobedience that would later define the full Civil Disobedience Movement of 1930.
🌱 Fig 4: The Tinkathia System
German dyes cheap→ Natural indigo unprofitable→ Tinkathia forced (3/20 land)→ Champaran Satyagraha '17→ Champaran Agrarian Act

Champaran Agrarian Act — What Changed

BeforeAfter the Act
Tinkathia (forced indigo on 3/20th land)Abolished
Abwab (extra taxes: house tax, grazing tax, irrigation tax — imposed since Mughal times)Suspended
📖 What exactly was Abwab? (tap to expand)
"Abwab" refers to any tax collected beyond the normal tax — a practice going back to Mughal times. Examples included house tax, grazing tax, and irrigation tax. The Champaran Agrarian Act suspended Abwab collections along with abolishing Tinkathia.

🎯 Exam Tip Champaran (1917) = Gandhiji's first Satyagraha in India — but it was not an all-India movement, only limited to Bihar!
❌ Common Mistake Tinkathia ≠ Abwab! Tinkathia = forced indigo cultivation on 3/20th of land. Abwab = extra taxes (house/grazing/irrigation) beyond the normal tax. The Champaran Agrarian Act dealt with both, separately.
🧠 Memory Trick "Tin-kathia" → think of the number 3 ("teen" in Hindi) out of 20 — forced indigo on 3/20th of the land!

Ahmedabad Mill Strike, Kheda & Bardoli Satyagraha

Ahmedabad Mill Strike (1918) — First Hunger Strike

The First World War (1914–1918) had pushed up prices sharply — even though the British-led Allied Powers won the war, the cost to Britain was enormous, and the effects of inflation were felt directly in India too.

  • Mill workers demanded a 50% wage increase, in line with inflation
  • Mill owners offered only 20%
  • Anusuya Sarabhai — sister of mill owner Ambalal Sarabhai — invited Gandhiji to Ahmedabad in 1918
  • This became Gandhiji's first hunger strike
👩 Anusuya & Ambalal Sarabhai (tap to expand)
Anusuya Sarabhai, who invited Gandhiji to intervene, was the sister of mill owner Ambalal Sarabhai — meaning Gandhiji was, in effect, mediating a dispute between a mill owner and his own sister's cause!
🎉 Fun FactAnusuya Sarabhai sided with the mill workers, even though her own brother Ambalal Sarabhai was one of the mill owners she was up against!

Kheda Satyagraha (1918) — First Non-Cooperation Movement

Kheda is in Gujarat. Under British rule, farmers had to pay land revenue — but if their produce fell below one-fourth of the normal amount, no revenue was payable at all.

  • Crop failure (drought) meant many farmers' produce fell well below the taxable threshold
  • Farmers demanded a full waiver of that year's revenue
  • The British insisted normal production had occurred, and refused any waiver
  • Gandhiji led the Kheda Satyagraha in support of these farmers

This is considered Gandhiji's first Non-Cooperation Movement.

🗺️ Fig 5: Gujarat — Where It All Happened

Bardoli Satyagraha (1928)

Bardoli, also in Gujarat, saw a similar land-tax Satyagraha in 1928 — but this one was not led by Gandhiji. It was led by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, who earned the title "Sardar" here, bestowed on him by the women participants of the movement.

📝 CGL Mains asked: "Which was Gandhiji's first Satyagraha AND first hunger strike?" — options included Champaran, Ahmedabad, Kheda & the Rowlatt Satyagraha.

The honest answer is actually both Champaran and Ahmedabad — Champaran (limited to Bihar, not an all-India movement) was the first Satyagraha, while the Ahmedabad Mill Strike was the first hunger strike.

🎯 Exam Tip Three "firsts" to learn together: Champaran = first Satyagraha, Ahmedabad = first hunger strike, Kheda = first Non-Cooperation Movement.
❌ Common Mistake Bardoli Satyagraha (1928) was not led by Gandhiji — it was Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel who led it, earning his title "Sardar" there!
🧠 Memory Trick Remember the order A-K-B: Ahmedabad (1918, wages) → Kheda (1918, land tax) → Bardoli (1928, land tax, Patel).
