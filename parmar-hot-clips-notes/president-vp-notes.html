The President — Articles 52 to 55

Article 52: "There shall be a President of India." This is the very first thing the Constitution establishes about the office.

Article 53: Executive Power of the Union — all the executive power of the Union is vested in the President.

🧠 Fig 1: Articles 52–55 at a Glance

Article 54 — Election: The Electoral College

Article 54 tells us who participates in the President's election — the Electoral College: elected MPs and elected MLAs only.

❌ Common Mistake Nominated members of Parliament (like nominated Rajya Sabha members) do NOT participate in the President's election — only elected MPs and MLAs form the Electoral College.

Article 55 — Manner of Election

Article 55 tells us how they are elected — the manner is Proportional Representation with Single Transferable Vote (PR-STV).

🧠 Memory Trick54 = WHO (Electoral College) → 55 = HOW (Manner: Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote)

Where Was It Borrowed From?

  • The offices of President and Vice President were borrowed from the USA
  • The manner/procedure of election (PR-STV) was borrowed from Ireland

This election is indirect — the people of India don't vote directly; their elected representatives (MPs/MLAs) vote on their behalf. Voting happens by secret ballot — nobody, not even one's own party, needs to be told who was voted for.

🎯 Exam Tip Nomination for the presidential election requires 50 proposers and 50 seconders — 50 people must propose the candidate's name, and 50 more must back (second) it.

Art 56–61: Term, Qualification & Impeachment

Article 58 — Qualification for Election as President

  • Must be a citizen of India
  • Must be at least 35 years of age
  • Must have the qualifications required to be elected as a member of the Lok Sabha (the Lok Sabha itself requires 25 years, but for President the age is separately fixed at 35)

Article 56 — Term of Office

The President's term is 5 years. If a vacancy arises due to death, resignation, or removal, and a new President is elected, they too serve a full fresh term of 5 years.

💡 Eligibility for Re-electionA President can be re-elected any number of times — unlike the USA, where a president can serve a maximum of only two terms.

Article 59 — Conditions of Office

The President must not be a Member of Parliament or a State Legislature. If already an MP/MLA, they must resign that seat before assuming the presidency.

Article 60 — Oath or Affirmation

The oath of the President is administered by the Chief Justice of India (CJI), and in the CJI's absence, by the senior-most judge of the Supreme Court available.

🧠 Fig 2: Order of Articles 56–61

Article 61 — Procedure for Impeachment

Impeachment means removal of the President. This system was borrowed from the USA Constitution. The ground for impeachment is violation of the Constitution.

  1. The impeachment motion can be initiated in either the Lok Sabha or the Rajya Sabha.
  2. It must be signed by 1/4th of the total members of whichever House it originates in, and a 14-day prior notice must be given.
  3. It must then be passed by a special majority — majority of the total membership of the House and 2/3rd of members present and voting (whichever is the bigger number is what's needed).
  4. It then goes to the other House, which investigates the charges — this makes the whole process quasi-judicial (the second House does judiciary-like work of examining charges, without actually being a court).
  5. The other House must also pass it by a special majority.
  6. The President has the full right to represent himself and explain why he should not be impeached.
🧠 Memory Trick Quasi-judicial = "half-baked" judiciary work — the second House is doing judiciary-like work (examining charges) but it isn't actually a court.
🎯 Exam Tip Sequence to remember: There shall be a President (52) → Executive Power (53) → Electoral College (54) → Manner of Election (55) → Term (56) → Qualification (58) → Conditions (59) → Oath (60) → Impeachment (61).

Electoral College vs Impeachment

A very important comparison — who participates in the President's election is NOT the same group as who participates in the President's impeachment.

PointElectoral College (Election)Impeachment (Removal)
MembersOnly elected MPs + elected MLAsAll MPs — elected AND nominated
MLAs' roleYes, MLAs participateNo role for MLAs at all
❌ Common Mistake Don't assume the same people who elect the President also impeach him! Election Electoral College = elected MPs + elected MLAs. Impeachment = ALL MPs (elected + nominated), no MLAs whatsoever.

MLAs come from Legislative Assemblies of all 28 states, plus the Union Territories of Delhi and Puducherry (which have their own Legislative Assemblies mentioned in the Constitution). Jammu & Kashmir has a Legislative Assembly too, but it is not yet mentioned in the Constitution for this purpose.

Article 62 — Filling a Vacancy

A vacancy in the President's office can be created by death, resignation, or removal. The resignation is given to the Vice President (e.g., Jagdeep Dhankhar's resignation was given to Droupadi Murmu).

🧠 Fig 3: Filling a Presidential Vacancy

Once the President is gone, the sitting Vice President becomes the Acting President (a temporary role), and a fresh election must be held within six months of the vacancy. If there is no Vice President either, the CJI takes over as Acting President.

📖 Example: When Zakir Hussain died in office, VP V.V. Giri became Acting President. When V.V. Giri resigned to contest the permanent presidential election himself, CJI M. Hidayatullah became Acting President — and after the new President took office, Hidayatullah went back to being CJI.

Quota & Value of Votes

  • Winning quota = Total valid votes ÷ (1 + number of vacancies), then add 1 — for the President's election there is only ONE vacancy, so the divisor is simply "1+1"
  • Value of an MLA's vote = (population of that state ÷ total number of elected MLAs in that state) × 1/1000
  • Value of an MP's vote = total value of all MLAs' votes (across all states) ÷ total number of elected MPs (no need to further divide by 1/1000 here)

The Vice President — Articles 63 to 71

Article 63: There shall be a Vice President of India. He is the ex-officio Chairman of the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) — meaning whoever becomes Vice President automatically becomes Rajya Sabha Chairman; there is no separate selection for that post.

During a casual vacancy of the President's office, the Vice President acts as President and discharges the President's functions.

🧠 Fig 4: Vice President — Key Facts

Election of Vice President

Also indirect, also by Proportional Representation + Single Transferable Vote. The Electoral College here is all Members of Parliament — both elected AND nominated. MLAs have no role in this election.

Article 67 — Term & Resignation

The Vice President's term is 5 years. If he wishes to resign, he resigns to the President (the President, by contrast, resigns to the Vice President).

Removal of the Vice President

  • The removal resolution can be initiated only in the Rajya Sabha (since he is its ex-officio Chairman)
  • It needs an effective majority in the Rajya Sabha (majority of the current effective strength, i.e., total minus vacancies)
  • It then goes to the Lok Sabha, where only a simple majority is needed
  • A 14-day prior notice is required, just like for the President
❌ Common Mistake Unlike the President's impeachment (which can start in either House), the Vice President's removal resolution can only start in the Rajya Sabha — because he's the Rajya Sabha's own ex-officio Chairman!

Article 68 & Article 70/71

Article 68 covers the time for holding an election to fill a Vice President vacancy and the term of office. Article 70 covers discharge of the President's functions in other contingencies (when the President is absent, not vacant). Article 71 says any dispute regarding the election of the President or Vice President is decided by the Supreme Court, whose decision is final.

Who administers the Vice President's oath? The President does.

🧠 Memory TrickV.V. Giri was the first Vice President to resign (to contest the presidential election). Jagdeep Dhankhar is the third Vice President to resign.
🎯 Exam Tip Hamid Ansari is the longest-serving Vice President — both he and S. Radhakrishnan served two terms (10 years each), but Ansari edges ahead when counting exact number of days.

Article 72 — Pardoning Power of the President

The President has the power to grant pardons under Article 72. There are five forms of this power:

🧠 Fig 5: Five Forms of Pardoning Power
FormMeaning
PardonComplete acquittal — fully absolves the person of all charges
CommutationChanging the form of punishment (e.g., death sentence → life imprisonment)
RemissionForm stays the same, only the duration is reduced (e.g., life imprisonment → 10 years)
RespiteReducing the term of punishment due to special conditions (e.g., deteriorating health)
ReprieveA temporary hold/stay on the execution of a punishment
🧠 Memory Trick Commutation changes the FORM (death → life). Remission changes only the DURATION, not the form (life imprisonment → 10 years). Don't mix the two up!
❌ Common Mistake Respite (reduction due to special/health conditions) is often confused with Remission (a general reduction in duration) — Respite is specifically tied to a special circumstance like health.

⭐ Exam-Ready Summary

  • Art 52-55: President exists, Executive Power, Electoral College (elected MPs+MLAs), Manner (PR-STV, from Ireland)
  • Art 56-61: Term (5 yrs, unlimited re-election), Qualification (35 yrs, citizen, Lok Sabha eligible), Conditions (no MP/MLA), Oath (by CJI), Impeachment (violation of Constitution, either House, special majority, quasi-judicial)
  • Art 62: Vacancy filled within 6 months; VP or CJI acts as President meanwhile
  • Art 63-71: VP is ex-officio RS Chairman; election by all MPs (elected+nominated), no MLAs; removal starts only in RS
  • Art 72: Pardon, Commutation, Remission, Respite, Reprieve
🎯 Exam Tip Remember the story chain in one breath: 52 President exists → 53 Executive Power → 54 Electoral College → 55 Manner of Election → 56 Term → 58 Qualification → 59 Conditions → 60 Oath → 61 Impeachment → 62 Vacancy → 63-71 Vice President → 72 Pardoning Power.
