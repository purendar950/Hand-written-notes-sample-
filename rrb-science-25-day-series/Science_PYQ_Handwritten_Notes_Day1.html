PHYSICS — GRAVITATION
Q1.
Who proposed the Universal Law of Gravitation?
(a) Galileo   (b) Newton   (c) Einstein   (d) Kepler
✔ Answer: (b) Sir Isaac Newton
  • Given in book "Principia Mathematica" in year 1687.
  • Formula: F = G·m₁m₂ / r²
  • r = distance between two objects.
  • If distance is halved → Force becomes 4 times (inverse square law).
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant, value = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg²
  • Value of G given by Henry Cavendish in 1798.
⚠ Common Mistake: Kepler gave Planetary Laws, NOT Law of Gravitation!
📌 Exam Tip: Einstein got Nobel Prize (1921) for Photoelectric Effect — NOT Relativity!

CHEMISTRY — CHEMICAL CHANGE / OXIDATION
Q2.
Rusting of iron is what type of reaction?
(a) Physical change   (b) Chemical change   (c) Neutralisation   (d) None of these
✔ Answer: Oxidation reaction (Chemical change)
  • Physical change: only state changes, reversible (ice → water).
  • Chemical change: new substance forms, irreversible (milk → curd).
  • Rusting = Fe + O₂ + H₂O → Fe₂O₃ (iron oxide / rust) — weight increases.
  • Other oxidation/corrosion examples: Copper → green coating of copper carbonate; Silver → black coating of Ag₂S (silver sulphide).
  • Prevent rust → Galvanisation (zinc coating) or Stainless Steel (add Chromium + Nickel).
  • Neutralisation: Acid + Base → Salt + Water (usually exothermic).
  • Candle burning = BOTH physical + chemical change together.
🧠 Memory Trick: OIL RIG → Oxidation Is Loss (of e⁻), Reduction Is Gain (of e⁻).

PHYSICS — LORENTZ FORCE
Q5.
When is the Lorentz force on a charged particle moving in a uniform magnetic field maximum?
✔ Answer: When particle moves perpendicular to the magnetic field
  • Lorentz Force: combined electric + magnetic force on a moving charge.
  • Formula: F = qvB sinθ, θ = angle between velocity & field.
  • Maximum: θ = 90° → sin90° = 1 → F = qvB (max)
  • Minimum (zero): θ = 0° or 180° (parallel/anti-parallel) → sin = 0

CHEMISTRY — CARBON ALLOTROPES
Q10.
Graphite conducts electricity but diamond does not. Main reason?
✔ Answer: Hybridisation — Graphite is sp², Diamond is sp³
  • Graphite: each C bonded to 3 carbons → sp² → slippery → used as lubricant.
  • Diamond: each C bonded to 4 carbons → sp³ → hardest natural substance (non-metal, since carbon is a non-metal).
  • Graphene: single layer, very thin.
  • Fullerene (Buckminsterfullerene): football-shaped, 60 carbon atoms (C₆₀).
  • Property of one C bonding to many C atoms = Catenation.

PHYSICS — SERIES & PARALLEL RESISTORS
Q11.
R₁ and R₂ in series give 25Ω; in parallel give 6Ω. Find R₁ and R₂.
✔ Answer: R₁ = 15Ω, R₂ = 10Ω
  • Series: R₁ + R₂ = 25
  • Parallel: R₁R₂ / (R₁+R₂) = 6 ⇒ R₁R₂ = 6 × 25 = 150
  • Solve: numbers whose sum=25, product=150 → 15 and 10
  • Series → same current, different p.d.  |  Parallel → same p.d., different current.

Q12.
Kepler's 2nd law is based on which conservation law?
(a) Linear momentum   (b) Angular momentum   (c) Energy   (d) Mass
✔ Answer: (b) Conservation of Angular Momentum

  • Conservation of angular momentum: if no external torque acts, total angular momentum stays constant.

CHEMISTRY — VAN'T HOFF FACTOR
Q13.
Which aqueous solution (same concentration) has the LOWEST freezing point?
✔ Answer: Al₂(SO₄)₃ (dissociates into 5 particles → highest Van't Hoff factor "i")
  • NaCl → 2 particles (Na⁺ + Cl⁻)
  • Glucose / Urea → 1 particle (does NOT dissociate)
  • Al₂(SO₄)₃ → 2 Al³⁺ + 3 SO₄²⁻ = 5 particles
  • More particles → higher Van't Hoff factor (i) → greater freezing point depression → lowest freezing point.

PHYSICS — EYE DEFECTS & LENSES
Q18.
What is the power (nature) of the lens used to correct Myopia (short-sightedness)?
✔ Answer: Concave lens, Negative power
  • Myopia: sees near objects, not far. Image forms before retina (eye lens power too high). Corrected by Concave lens (diverging, −ve power).
  • Hypermetropia: sees far, not near. Image forms behind retina. Corrected by Convex lens (converging, +ve power).
  • Presbyopia: lens loses flexibility with age → needs Bifocal lens (convex below for near + concave above for far).
  • Cataract: membrane forms over lens, removed by surgery.
  • Glaucoma: fluid pressure ↑ inside eye → damages optic nerve → vision loss (often hereditary, irreversible).
  • Trachoma: caused by bacteria.
🧠 Memory Trick: Convex/Convex mirror = always +ve focal length & power. Concave/Concave mirror = always −ve.

CHEMISTRY — pH SCALE
Q26.
If pH increases from 3 to 6, what happens to [H⁺] concentration?
✔ Answer: [H⁺] decreases 1000 times

  • Acid dissolved in water gives H⁺, which combines with water → H₃O⁺ (Hydronium ion)

CHEMISTRY — REACTIVITY SERIES / DISPLACEMENT
Q37.
What happens to the colour of Copper Sulphate solution when a piece of Zinc is placed in it?
✔ Answer: Blue colour fades / becomes colourless (Zinc displaces Copper)
  • Zinc is more reactive than Copper (higher in reactivity series) → displaces Cu from CuSO₄.
  • Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu (this is a Displacement Reaction)
  • If an Iron nail is placed in CuSO₄ → reddish-brown copper metal deposits on the nail (Fe is also above Cu in reactivity series).
  • Rule: A more reactive metal (above) can always displace a less reactive one (below) — never the reverse.

BIOLOGY — HUMAN DIGESTIVE SYSTEM
Q49.
In which organ does most digestion & absorption of food take place?
(a) Stomach   (b) Large intestine   (c) Small intestine   (d) Liver
✔ Answer: (c) Small Intestine (length 7-8 m)
Path of food (Alimentary canal):
Fig 17 — Alimentary Canal: Path of Food
  • Salivary amylase: complex carbs → simple carbs (in mouth).
  • Gastric juice (stomach): HCl + Pepsin (breaks protein → amino acids), works in acidic medium (pH ~1.6). Mucus membrane protects stomach lining.
  • Bile juice (Liver, stored in Gallbladder): makes medium basic + emulsifies fat (breaks big fat globules → smaller).
  • Pancreatic juice: Trypsin (protein → amino acids, in basic medium) + Lipase (fat → fatty acids).
  • Villi in small intestine → increase surface area for absorption.
  • Liver = largest gland in body; Pancreas = 2nd largest gland (both NOT part of alimentary canal — food doesn't pass through them).
  • Small intestine length: 7-8 m; Large intestine: 1-2 m (shorter but wider).
  • Food stored in body as Glycogen (animals); plants store as Starch.
📌 Exam Tip: Sequence — Mouth → Pharynx → Oesophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Rectum → Anus.

PHYSICS — KINETIC ENERGY
Q50.
If mass of a moving body is doubled (velocity constant), what happens to Kinetic Energy?
✔ Answer: KE also doubles
  • KE = ½mv² → KE ∝ m (directly) → mass doubled ⇒ KE doubled.
  • If velocity doubled (mass constant) → KE becomes 4 times (v² term).
  • Relation with linear momentum (p): KE = p²/2m
  • If momentum (p) doubled → KE becomes 4 times (p² term).
  • Mechanical Energy = KE + PE
