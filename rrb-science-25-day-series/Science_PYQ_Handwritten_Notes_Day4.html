BIOLOGY — PHOTOSYNTHESIS
Q1.
Which gas is released during photosynthesis in plants?
✔ Answer: Oxygen
  • Occurs in the chloroplast of the plastid. Two stages: Light reaction (in the grana/thylakoids) and Dark reaction (in the stroma — the Calvin Cycle).
  • Thylakoid stacks = grana. Dark reaction does NOT need light directly, but depends on products of the light reaction.

PHYSICS — WORK DONE (SIGN CONVENTION)
Q2.
If a force F is applied to an object in the direction of its motion, the work done will be:
✔ Answer: Positive
  • W = Fs cosθ — θ is the angle between force and displacement.
  • Displacement same direction as force (θ=0°, cos0=1) → Work positive.
  • Displacement opposite to force (θ=180°) → Work negative.
  • No displacement, or θ=90° (force ⊥ displacement, cos90=0) → Work zero.

BIOLOGY — GLANDS OF THE BODY
Q4.
Which of the following glands is the largest in the human body?
✔ Answer: Liver — largest gland overall (largest endocrine gland = Thyroid)
  • Endocrine glands → ductless, secrete hormones directly into blood.
  • Exocrine glands → secrete enzymes through a duct.
  • Pancreas = mixed gland (both endocrine + exocrine).
  • Liver — largest gland, exocrine, reddish-brown, can regenerate (has special Kupffer cells); releases bile juice (stored in gallbladder) into the small intestine — makes medium basic + emulsifies fat. NOT part of the alimentary canal (food never enters it).
  • Hepatitis — viral disease affecting the liver.
  • Thyroid — largest endocrine gland; secretes Thyroxine (T4) — regulates body metabolism.
⚠ Don't confuse "largest gland" (Liver) with "largest endocrine gland" (Thyroid) — read the question carefully!

PHYSICS — FORCE = MASS × ACCELERATION
Q5.
An object has mass 10 kg and acceleration 2 m/s². What is the force acting on it?
✔ Answer: 20 N
  • F = m × a = 10 × 2 = 20 Newton. Simple direct substitution.
CHEMISTRY — NONMETALS (PHYSICAL STATE)
Q6.
Which nonmetal exists in the liquid state at room temperature?
✔ Answer: Bromine (Br₂)
  • Mercury = only metal liquid at room temperature (Francium, Cesium also have very low melting points).
  • Chlorine has two isotopes: 35Cl and 37Cl in a 3:1 ratio → average atomic mass = 35.5.
  • Chlorine gas has a pungent smell. Iodine isotopes are used in goitre treatment.

BIOLOGY — PLANT TISSUES (OVERVIEW)
Q7.
Which tissue transports food in plants? (Plant tissue types — overview)
✔ Answer for Q7: Phloem
  • Meristematic tissue (dividing/growing cells): Apical (at root/shoot tips → height), Intercalary (at nodes → height), Lateral (in stem girth → width).
  • Meristematic cells: dense cytoplasm, prominent nucleus, no vacuole; divide continuously, then differentiate into permanent tissue.
  • Permanent tissue → Simple: Parenchyma (food storage), Collenchyma (flexibility — irregular thickening at corners), Sclerenchyma (rigidity/toughness, e.g. coconut husk).
  • Permanent tissue → Complex: Xylem (water) and Phloem (food) — see next page.

PHYSICS — ELECTRICAL MEASURING INSTRUMENTS
Q8.
Which instrument is used to measure electric current?
✔ Answer: Ammeter (connected in series)
  • Voltmeter → measures potential difference, connected in parallel (same PD, different current in each branch).
  • Ohmmeter → measures resistance. Resistivity (ρ) = specific resistance, depends on the nature of the material.
BIOLOGY — TRANSPIRATION
Q9.
Transpiration in plants mainly takes place through which structure?
✔ Answer: Stomata (tiny pores in leaf epidermis, guarded by kidney-shaped guard cells)
  • Transpiration = water loss through stomata, occurs during the day.
  • Guttation = water loss at night, through hydathodes at leaf edges.

PHYSICS — ELECTRIC FUSE
Q10.
What is the primary function of a fuse in an electrical circuit?
✔ Answer: To prevent circuits/appliances from excess current (by melting/breaking the circuit)
  • Fuse wire needs: low melting point + high resistance — melts quickly on current overflow.
  • Contrast — bulb filament (tungsten) needs a high melting point, so it can glow without melting.

PHYSICS — UNIFORM SPEED & CIRCULAR MOTION
Q12.
If an object moves with uniform speed, what is its acceleration?
✔ Answer: Zero (velocity is not changing)
  • In Uniform Circular Motion (UCM), speed is constant, but direction keeps changing → velocity changes at every point → acceleration exists.
  • This is centripetal acceleration, directed towards the centre: a = v²/r. Corresponding centripetal force = mv²/r.

PHYSICS — GRAVITY ON THE MOON
Q14.
Approximately how much does an object weigh on the Moon compared to Earth?
✔ Answer: 1/6th of its weight on Earth
  • Moon's gravity ≈ 1/6th of Earth's gravity → weight (= mg) also becomes 1/6th. Mass stays the same everywhere.
CHEMISTRY — ORGANIC vs INORGANIC COMPOUNDS
Q15.
Which of the following is NOT an organic compound? (options included ethanol, acetic acid, sodium carbonate)
✔ Answer: Sodium carbonate (Na₂CO₃) — inorganic
  • Organic compounds have a C–H bond — present in ethanol & acetic acid, absent in Na₂CO₃.
  • Na₂CO₃·10H₂O = washing soda — removes permanent hardness of water; used in glass factories.
  • Baking soda (NaHCO₃) — antacid, fire extinguisher, baking industry; reacts with mild edible acid (tartaric acid) → releases CO₂.
  • Acetic acid (CH₃COOH) — used as preservative; also called glacial acetic acid (freezes just below room temp). Sodium benzoate = another common preservative.

BIOLOGY — PARENCHYMA VARIANTS
Q16.
Which tissue is responsible for the transport of water in plants?

CHEMISTRY — METAL & NON-METAL OXIDES
Q19.
What is the general nature of metal oxides?
✔ Answer: Basic (non-metal oxides are generally acidic)
  • Alkali metals (Group 1: Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs) — their oxides are basic AND dissolve in water → called alkalis. (All alkalis are bases, but not all bases are alkalis.)
  • Metal oxide + Acid → Salt + Water. Non-metal oxide + Base → Salt + Water.
  • Amphoteric oxides (react with BOTH acids & bases): ZnO, Al₂O₃, SnO, BeO.
🧠 Memory Trick: "Sn-Be-Zn-Al" (Tin, Beryllium, Zinc, Aluminium oxides) → all amphoteric.

PHYSICS — LENS POWER
Q26.
If the focal length of a convex lens decreases, what happens to its power?
✔ Answer: Power increases
  • P = 1/f (f in metres) → unit = Diopter (D). Power ∝ 1/f — inverse relation.
  • Convex lens/mirror → focal length always positive. Concave lens/mirror → negative.
  • Convex lens → treats hypermetropia. Concave lens → treats myopia. Plane mirror → f = ∞ → Power = 0.

CHEMISTRY — REACTIVITY SERIES
Q40.
Which of the following metals is the most reactive?
✔ Answer: Potassium (K)
  • Order (most → least reactive): K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au.
  • In a displacement reaction, a metal higher in the series can displace one below it.
  • K and Na are so reactive they react with cold water itself (exothermically). Metals lower down need hot water, then steam, to react.
  • Gold/Silver are least reactive → don't tarnish easily → used in jewellery.
🧠 Memory Trick (as taught in class): "Katrina Alto-Zen-Ferrari" → K-Na-Ca-Mg-Al-Zn-Fe (top half, most reactive), then Pb-Cu-Hg-Ag-Au (bottom half, least reactive).
