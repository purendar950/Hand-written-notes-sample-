⚡ PHYSICS — Gravitation
Q1
Who proposed the Universal Law of Gravitation?
aGalileo bNewton cEinstein dKepler
✔Sir Isaac Newton
Inverse-square law
  • Given in book "Principia Mathematica" in 1687.
  • Formula: F = G·m₁m₂ / r²  (r = distance between two bodies).
  • Force & distance are inversely related → halve distance = 4× force.
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant = 6.67×10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg².
  • Value of G given by Henry Cavendish (1798).
⚠ Common MistakeKepler gave Planetary Laws, NOT the Law of Gravitation.
📌 Exam TipEinstein won the Nobel (1921) for the Photoelectric Effect — not Relativity.
🧪 CHEMISTRY — Types of Reactions
Q2
Rusting of iron is what type of reaction?
aPhysical change bChemical change cNeutralisation dNone
✔Oxidation reaction (a Chemical change)
  • Physical change: only state changes, reversible (ice → water).
  • Chemical change: new substance, irreversible (milk → curd).
  • Rusting: Fe + O₂ + H₂O → Fe₂O₃ (rust); weight increases.
  • Corrosion: Copper → green copper carbonate; Silver → black Ag₂S.
  • Prevent rust → Galvanisation (zinc) or Stainless Steel (Cr + Ni).
  • Neutralisation: Acid + Base → Salt + Water (usually exothermic).
  • Candle burning = both physical + chemical change.
🧠 Memory TrickOIL RIG → Oxidation Is Loss (e⁻), Reduction Is Gain (e⁻).

Q4
Oxidation state of Oxygen in Oxygen Difluoride (OF₂)?
a−2 b+2 c−1 d+1
✔+2
  • Let O = x; molecule neutral ⇒ total = 0.
  • Fluorine (most electronegative halogen) is always −1.
  • x + 2(−1) = 0 ⇒ x = +2.
⚠ Common MistakeOxygen is usually −2, but with Fluorine it becomes +2.
Q5
When is the Lorentz force on a charged particle (in a uniform magnetic field) maximum?
✔When the particle moves perpendicular to the field
F max when v ⊥ B
  • Lorentz Force = electric + magnetic force on a moving charge.
  • F = qvB sinθ  (θ = angle between v and B).
  • Max: θ = 90° → sin90° = 1 → F = qvB.
  • Zero: θ = 0°/180° (parallel / anti-parallel).

🧪 CHEMISTRY — Allotropes of Carbon
Q10
Graphite conducts electricity but diamond does not. Reason = hybridisation of carbon in graphite?
✔Graphite = sp²  |  Diamond = sp³
Layers vs 3-D network
  • Graphite: 1 C bonded to 3 C → sp² → slippery → lubricant; conducts.
  • Diamond: 1 C bonded to 4 C → sp³ → hardest natural substance (a non-metal); no conduction.
  • Graphene = single thin layer; Fullerene (C₆₀) = football-shaped.
  • 1 C bonding many C atoms = property of Catenation.
⚡ PHYSICS — Circuits & Kepler
Q11
R₁ and R₂ give 25Ω in series and 6Ω in parallel. Find R₁ and R₂.
✔R₁ = 15Ω, R₂ = 10Ω
  • Series ⇒ R₁ + R₂ = 25.
  • Parallel ⇒ R₁R₂/(R₁+R₂) = 6 ⇒ R₁R₂ = 6×25 = 150.
  • Sum 25, product 150 → 15 and 10.
  • Series → same current; Parallel → same p.d.
Q12
Kepler's 2nd law is based on which conservation law?
aLinear momentum bAngular momentum cEnergy dMass
✔Conservation of Angular Momentum
Law of Areas
  • 1st (Orbits): elliptical orbit, Sun at one focus.
  • 2nd (Areas): sweeps equal area in equal time (angular momentum).
  • 3rd (Time Period): T² ∝ r³.
  • No external torque ⇒ angular momentum constant.
Q13
Which aqueous solution (same concentration) has the LOWEST freezing point?
✔Al₂(SO₄)₃ — gives most particles (highest "i")
  • NaCl → 2 particles; Glucose/Urea → 1 (no dissociation).
  • Al₂(SO₄)₃ → 2 Al³⁺ + 3 SO₄²⁻ = 5 particles.
  • More particles → higher van't Hoff factor (i) → more freezing-point drop → lowest FP.

👁 PHYSICS — Eye Defects & Radioactivity
Q18
Power (nature) of the lens used to correct Myopia (short-sightedness)?
✔Concave lens — Negative power
Myopia vs Hypermetropia
  • Myopia: near OK, far blurred; image before retina → Concave lens (−ve).
  • Hypermetropia: far OK, near blurred; image behind retina → Convex lens (+ve).
  • Presbyopia: age-related; needs Bifocal lens.
  • Cataract: lens membrane → surgery. Glaucoma: eye pressure ↑ → optic-nerve damage. Trachoma: bacterial.
🧠 Memory TrickConvex lens/mirror → always +ve; Concave → always −ve.

If pH increases from 3 to 6, what happens to [H⁺] concentration?

🦠 BIOLOGY — Human Digestive System
Q49
In which organ does most digestion & absorption of food take place?
aStomach bLarge intestine cSmall intestine dLiver
✔Small Intestine (~7-8 m)
Alimentary canal — path of food
  • Salivary amylase (mouth): complex carbs → simple carbs.
  • Gastric juice: HCl + Pepsin (protein → amino acids), acidic pH ~1.6; mucus protects lining.
  • Bile (Liver → Gallbladder): makes basic + emulsifies fat.
  • Pancreatic juice: Trypsin (protein) + Lipase (fat → fatty acids).
  • Villi increase surface area for absorption.
  • Liver = largest gland; Pancreas = 2nd largest (both NOT in alimentary canal).
  • Small intestine ~7-8 m; Large intestine ~1-2 m.
  • Stored as Glycogen (animals); plants store as Starch.
📌 SequenceMouth → Pharynx → Oesophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Rectum → Anus.
⚡ PHYSICS — Kinetic Energy
Q50
If the mass of a moving body is doubled (velocity constant), what happens to its KE?
✔KE also doubles
  • KE = ½mv² → KE ∝ m → mass doubled ⇒ KE doubled.
  • If velocity doubled → KE = 4× (v² term).
  • Momentum relation: KE = p²/2m; p doubled → KE = 4×.
  • Mechanical Energy = KE + PE.
