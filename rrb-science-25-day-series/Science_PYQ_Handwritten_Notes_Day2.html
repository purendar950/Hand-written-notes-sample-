CHEMISTRY — pH OF HUMAN BLOOD
Q3.
What is the normal pH of human blood?
(a) 6.4   (b) 7.0   (c) 7.4   (d) 8.4
✔ Answer: (c) 7.4 (slightly basic/alkaline)
  • Human body normally functions in pH range 7.3 – 7.6.
  • Saliva pH ≈ 6.4 (slightly acidic).
  • Dilution Rule: Always add Acid to Water (slowly), NEVER Water to Acid — reaction is highly exothermic!
  • Tooth decay begins when mouth pH drops below 5.5.
  • Acid rain: when rain pH drops below 5.5.
  • Milk of Magnesia (antacid) = Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda), pH ≈ 10.
  • NaOH → pH = 14 (strongest base on the scale).
  • Stomach acid = HCl (more acidic than lemon juice).

PHYSICS — SPEED OF LIGHT & SOUND
Q5.
In which medium is the speed of light the highest?
(a) Glass   (b) Water   (c) Diamond   (d) Vacuum
✔ Answer: (d) Vacuum — speed = 3×10⁸ m/s
  • Higher refractive index (denser) ⇒ lower speed of light. Diamond (n=2.42) → lowest speed among common media.
  • Rarer → Denser: light bends toward normal, speed decreases.
  • Denser → Rarer: light bends away from normal, speed increases.
Speed of Sound:
  • Normal speed of sound = 343 m/s at 20°C (330 m/s at 0°C) — increases with temperature.
  • Order: Solid > Liquid > Gas

PHYSICS — LIGHT: REFLECTION, REFRACTION, DISPERSION
Q7.
What is the phenomenon called when white light splits into 7 colours after passing through a prism?
(a) Reflection   (b) Refraction   (c) Dispersion   (d) Scattering
✔ Answer: (c) Dispersion of light — first explained by Newton using a prism
  • Laws of Reflection: (1) Incident ray, reflected ray & normal lie in the same plane. (2) ∠incidence = ∠reflection.
  • Refraction: Rarer→Denser: bends toward normal, speed↓. Denser→Rarer: bends away from normal, speed↑.
Fig 3 — Dispersion through a Prism
  • VIBGYOR: Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange, Red
  • Violet — shortest wavelength, max deviation/refraction.
  • Red — longest wavelength, min deviation → used for danger signals (travels far without deviating).
  • Rainbow forms via: 2 × Refraction + 1 × Total Internal Reflection (inside a water drop).
  • Scattering: Sky appears blue (blue scatters most); Sun appears red at sunrise/sunset; Sky appears black to astronauts in space (no atmosphere to scatter).
Fig 4 — Rainbow: Refraction + TIR + Refraction

PHYSICS — AMMETER vs VOLTMETER
Q8.
Which instrument measures electric current, and how is it connected?
✔ Answer: Ammeter, connected in Series
  • Ammeter → measures current → connected in Series (current same in series).
  • Voltmeter → measures potential difference → connected in Parallel (p.d. same in parallel).
  • Galvanometer → measures very low current, shows magnitude + direction.
  • Series: R = R₁+R₂+...  |  Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂+...

PHYSICS — COMBINATION OF LENSES
Q12.
Two lenses of +2D and −1.5D are placed in contact. Find focal length of the combination.
✔ Answer: f = 2 m
  • Net Power = P₁ + P₂ + ... = 2 + (−1.5) = 0.5 D
  • f = 1/P = 1/0.5 = 2 m
  • +ve power = convex lens (used for Hypermetropia); −ve power = concave lens (used for Myopia)
PHYSICS — OHM'S LAW: RESISTIVITY
Q13.
Verifying Ohm's law: V on Y-axis, I on X-axis. What does the slope represent?
(a) Conductance   (b) Resistance   (c) Electric power   (d) Resistivity
✔ Answer: (b) Resistance
  • R = ρL/A  →  ρ (rho) = Resistivity/Specific resistance — depends only on material, not on length/area.
  • SI unit: Resistance = Ohm (Ω); Resistivity = Ohm-metre (Ωm); Conductance = 1/R, unit = Siemens.
  • Copper has low resistivity → used in wires (allows current to flow easily).
  • Fuse wire needs: High resistance + Low melting point → heats up fast & melts to break circuit (protects appliances) — based on Joule's Heating Effect.
PHYSICS — FLEMING'S LEFT-HAND RULE
Q14.

✔ Answer: Magnetic Field
Fig 6 — Fleming's Left Hand Rule
🧠 Memory Trick: "Mother-Father-Child" → Thumb=Motion(Force), Fore(index)finger=Field, Middle=Current.

  • Right Hand Rule → used for Generators (finds induced Current direction when conductor moves in a changing magnetic field).

CHEMISTRY — HOMOLOGOUS SERIES
Q16.
What is the difference in molecular mass between any two consecutive members of a homologous series?
✔ Answer: CH₂ = 14 (12+2×1)
  • Homologous series = compounds with same functional group, similar chemical properties, each member differs by CH₂ (14 u).
  • E.g. Alkanes: Methane(CH₄)→Ethane(C₂H₆)→Propane(C₃H₈)→Butane(C₄H₁₀) — each step +CH₂.
  • Saturated (Alkanes): burn with clean blue flame.
  • Unsaturated (Alkenes/Alkynes): burn with yellow, sooty flame (incomplete combustion).

PHYSICS — CALORIMETRY (Ice + Water)
Q23.
100g ice at 0°C mixed with 100g water at 100°C. Find final temperature.
✔ Answer: 10°C
  • Heat gained by ice = Heat lost by water
  • Ice: melts first (latent heat, L=80 cal/g) then warms up → mL + msΔT
  • 100(80) + 100(1)(Tf−0) = 100(1)(100−Tf)
  • 8000 + 100Tf = 10000 − 100Tf ⇒ 200Tf = 2000 ⇒ Tf = 10°C

CHEMISTRY — FARADAY'S LAWS OF ELECTROLYSIS
Q25.
According to Faraday's First Law of Electrolysis, mass (M) of substance deposited at an electrode is directly proportional to?
✔ Answer: Total electric charge passed (Q = It)
  • 1st Law: M ∝ Q (Charge = Current × Time)
  • 2nd Law: Same charge through different electrolytes → mass deposited ∝ their equivalent weight.

BIOLOGY — GAS EXCHANGE IN PLANTS
Q36.
Through which structure does gaseous exchange take place primarily in plants?
(a) Xylem   (b) Stomata   (c) Stem   (d) Root
✔ Answer: (b) Stomata

  • Phloem: transports food (two-way/bidirectional).

BIOLOGY — HUMAN HEART & CIRCULATION
Q39.
The Tricuspid valve regulates blood flow between which two chambers of the heart?
✔ Answer: Right Atrium and Right Ventricle
  • Arteries: carry oxygenated blood (heart→body). Exception: Pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood (heart→lungs).
  • Veins: carry deoxygenated blood (body→heart). Exception: Pulmonary vein carries oxygenated blood (lungs→heart).
  • Human heart = 4 chambers (2 Atria + 2 Ventricles). Birds=4; Reptiles=3 (Crocodile=4); Amphibians=3; Fish=2.
  • Superior Vena Cava: deoxygenated blood from upper body. Inferior Vena Cava (largest vein): from lower body.
  • Pericardium = outer covering of heart (like Pleura for lungs). Septum = wall separating left & right heart.
  • SA node (Sino-atrial node, in right atrium) = natural pacemaker of the heart.
Fig 12 — Blood Circulation through the Heart
