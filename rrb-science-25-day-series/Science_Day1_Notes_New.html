PHYSICS — GRAVITATION
Q1.
Who proposed the Universal Law of Gravitation?
(a) Galileo    (b) Newton    (c) Einstein    (d) Kepler
✔ Answer: (b) Sir Isaac Newton
Fig 1 — Inverse Square Law
  • Given in the book "Principia Mathematica" in year 1687.
  • Formula: F = G·m₁m₂ / r²  (r = distance between two bodies).
  • Force & distance are inversely proportional (inverse-square).
  • If distance is halved → Force becomes 4 times.
  • G = Universal Gravitational Constant = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg².
  • Value of G given by Henry Cavendish in 1798.
⚠ Common Mistake: Kepler gave the Planetary Laws, NOT the Law of Gravitation.
✅ Exam Tip: Einstein won the Nobel Prize (1921) for the Photoelectric Effect — NOT for Relativity.
CHEMISTRY — TYPES OF REACTIONS
Q2.
Rusting of iron is what type of reaction?
(a) Physical change    (b) Chemical change    (c) Neutralisation    (d) None of these
✔ Answer: Oxidation reaction (a Chemical change)
  • Physical change: only state changes, reversible (ice → water).
  • Chemical change: new substance forms, irreversible (milk → curd).
  • Rusting: Fe + O₂ + H₂O → Fe₂O₃ (iron oxide / rust); weight increases.
  • Other corrosion: Copper → green coat of copper carbonate; Silver → black coat of Ag₂S.
  • Rust prevention → Galvanisation (zinc coat) or Stainless Steel (add Chromium + Nickel).
  • Neutralisation: Acid + Base → Salt + Water (generally exothermic).
  • Candle burning = BOTH physical + chemical change together.
🧠 Memory Trick: OIL RIG → Oxidation Is Loss (of e⁻), Reduction Is Gain (of e⁻).

CHEMISTRY — OXIDATION STATE
Q4.
What is the oxidation state of Oxygen in Oxygen Difluoride (OF₂)?
(a) −2    (b) +2    (c) −1    (d) +1
✔ Answer: (b) +2

PHYSICS — LORENTZ FORCE
Q5.
When is the Lorentz force on a charged particle moving in a uniform magnetic field maximum?
✔ Answer: When the particle moves perpendicular to the magnetic field.
Fig 2 — F max when v ⊥ B
  • Lorentz Force = combined electric + magnetic force on a moving charge.
  • Formula: F = qvB sinθ  (θ = angle between v and B).
  • Maximum: θ = 90° → sin90° = 1 → F = qvB.
  • Minimum (zero): θ = 0° or 180° (parallel / anti-parallel) → sin = 0.

Q9.
Double fertilisation is a unique feature of angiosperms. What is formed by triple fusion?
(a) Embryo    (b) Cotyledon    (c) Endosperm    (d) Seed coat

CHEMISTRY — ALLOTROPES OF CARBON
Q10.
Graphite conducts electricity but diamond does not. The main reason is the hybridisation of carbon atoms in graphite. What is it?
✔ Answer: Graphite is sp²; Diamond is sp³.
Fig 3 — Layers vs 3D network
  • Graphite: each C bonded to 3 carbons → sp² → slippery → used as lubricant; good conductor.
  • Diamond: each C bonded to 4 carbons → sp³ → hardest natural substance (a non-metal); poor conductor.
  • Graphene: single layer, very thin.
  • Fullerene (Buckminsterfullerene): football-shaped, 60 carbon atoms (C₆₀).
  • One C bonding with many C atoms = property of Catenation.

PHYSICS — SERIES & PARALLEL RESISTORS
Q11.
R₁ and R₂ give 25Ω in series and 6Ω in parallel. Find R₁ and R₂.
✔ Answer: R₁ = 15Ω, R₂ = 10Ω.
Fig 4 — Series vs Parallel
  • Series ⇒ R₁ + R₂ = 25.
  • Parallel ⇒ R₁R₂ / (R₁+R₂) = 6 ⇒ R₁R₂ = 6 × 25 = 150.
  • Two numbers: sum = 25, product = 150 → 15 and 10.
  • Series → same current, different p.d.  |  Parallel → same p.d., different current.
PHYSICS — KEPLER'S LAWS
Q12.
Kepler's second law is based on which conservation law?
(a) Linear momentum    (b) Angular momentum    (c) Energy    (d) Mass
✔ Answer: (b) Conservation of Angular Momentum.
Fig 5 — Law of Areas
  • 1st Law (Law of Orbits): planets move in an elliptical orbit, Sun at one focus.
  • 2nd Law (Law of Areas): planet sweeps equal areas in equal time — based on angular momentum conservation.
  • 3rd Law (Law of Time Period): T² ∝ r³.
  • If no external torque acts, total angular momentum stays constant.
CHEMISTRY — VAN'T HOFF FACTOR
Q13.
Which aqueous solution (all same concentration) has the LOWEST freezing point?
✔ Answer: Al₂(SO₄)₃ — gives most particles (highest van't Hoff factor "i").
  • NaCl → 2 particles (Na⁺ + Cl⁻).
  • Glucose / Urea → 1 particle (does NOT dissociate).
  • Al₂(SO₄)₃ → 2 Al³⁺ + 3 SO₄²⁻ = 5 particles.
  • More particles → higher van't Hoff factor (i) → more freezing-point depression → lowest freezing point.

PHYSICS — EYE DEFECTS & LENSES
Q18.
What is the power (nature) of the lens used to correct Myopia (short-sightedness)?
✔ Answer: Concave lens — Negative power.
  • Myopia: near objects visible, far not. Image forms before the retina (eye-lens power too high). Corrected by Concave lens (diverging, −ve power).
  • Hypermetropia: far visible, near not. Image forms behind the retina. Corrected by Convex lens (converging, +ve power).
  • Presbyopia: lens loses flexibility with age → needs Bifocal lens (convex below for near + concave above for far).
  • Cataract: membrane forms on lens → removed by surgery.
  • Glaucoma: eye fluid pressure ↑ → damages optic nerve → vision loss (often hereditary, irreversible).
  • Trachoma: caused by bacteria.
🧠 Memory Trick: Convex lens / Convex mirror → always +ve focal length & power. Concave → always −ve.

Q21.
If Earth's radius decreases by 1% (mass constant), effect on 'g'?
✔ Answer: g increases by 2%.

Q24.
Lead nitrate heated in a test tube gives brown fumes. Which gas is responsible?
✔ Answer: NO₂ (Nitrogen dioxide) — the brown fumes.

CHEMISTRY — pH SCALE
Q26.
If pH increases from 3 to 6, what happens to the [H⁺] ion concentration?
✔ Answer: [H⁺] decreases 1000 times.
  • pH = −log[H⁺];  German "p" = potenz (power).
  • pH 3 → [H⁺] = 10⁻³ mol/L;   pH 6 → [H⁺] = 10⁻⁶ mol/L.
  • Ratio = 10⁻³ / 10⁻⁶ = 10³ = 1000 times decrease.
  • Acid in water gives H⁺, which joins water → H₃O⁺ (Hydronium ion).

BIOLOGY — HOMOLOGOUS vs ANALOGOUS ORGANS
Q34.
Which is a correct example of Homologous organs?

✔ Answer: (b) Potato eye & Sweet-potato eye.

CHEMISTRY — REACTIVITY SERIES / DISPLACEMENT
Q37.
What happens to the colour of Copper Sulphate solution when a piece of Zinc is placed in it?
✔ Answer: Blue colour fades / becomes colourless (Zinc displaces Copper).
  • Zinc is more reactive than Copper (higher in reactivity series) → displaces Cu from CuSO₄.
  • Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu  (a Displacement Reaction).
  • An iron nail in CuSO₄ → reddish-brown copper deposits on nail (Fe also above Cu).
  • Rule: a more reactive metal (above) displaces a less reactive one (below) — never the reverse.
🧠 Reactivity Series (high→low): K Na Ca Mg Al Zn Fe H Cu Ag Au.

BIOLOGY — HUMAN DIGESTIVE SYSTEM
Q49.
In which organ does most digestion & absorption of food take place?
(a) Stomach    (b) Large intestine    (c) Small intestine    (d) Liver
✔ Answer: (c) Small Intestine (length ~7-8 m).
Fig 15 — Alimentary Canal: Path of Food
  • Salivary amylase (mouth): complex carbs → simple carbs.
  • Gastric juice (stomach): HCl + Pepsin (protein → amino acids), works in acidic medium (pH ~1.6). Mucus membrane protects the lining.
  • Bile juice (Liver, stored in Gallbladder): makes medium basic + emulsifies fat (big fat globules → smaller).
  • Pancreatic juice: Trypsin (protein → amino acids, basic medium) + Lipase (fat → fatty acids).
  • Villi in small intestine → increase surface area for absorption.
  • Liver = largest gland in body; Pancreas = 2nd largest (both NOT part of the alimentary canal — food doesn't pass through them).
  • Small intestine ~7-8 m; Large intestine ~1-2 m (shorter but wider).
  • Food stored as Glycogen (in animals); plants store as Starch.
✅ Sequence: Mouth → Pharynx → Oesophagus → Stomach → Small Intestine → Large Intestine → Rectum → Anus.

PHYSICS — KINETIC ENERGY
Q50.
If the mass of a moving body is doubled (velocity constant), what happens to its Kinetic Energy?
✔ Answer: KE also doubles.
  • KE = ½mv² → KE ∝ m → mass doubled ⇒ KE doubled.
  • If velocity doubled (mass constant) → KE becomes 4 times (v² term).
  • Relation with linear momentum (p): KE = p²/2m.
  • If momentum (p) doubled → KE becomes 4 times (p² term).
  • Mechanical Energy = KE + PE.
