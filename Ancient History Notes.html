🕉️ Section 1 — Vedic Period & Upanishads
Q1. In which epic is it mentioned that dying during Uttarayan leads to salvation?
A
Ramayana
B
Mahabharata ✔
C
Vedas
D
Upanishads
✅ Answer: B — Mahabharata. Uttarayan is the northward movement of the sun (from Tropic of Capricorn to Tropic of Cancer). The Bhagavad Gita (part of Mahabharata) states that dying during Uttarayan leads to moksha. The sun moves between Tropic of Cancer (23½° N) and Tropic of Capricorn (23½° S). Uttarayan period: January to June/mid-July. Makar Sankranti celebrates the beginning of Uttarayan. June 21 = International Yoga Day (sun on Tropic of Cancer). December 22 = World Meditation Day (sun on Tropic of Capricorn). Author of Mahabharata: Ved Vyas.
Q2. What does the term 'Upanishad' literally mean?
A
Speak loudly
B
Write in good handwriting
C
Meditate quietly
D
Sit close together ✔
✅ Answer: D — Sit close together. The student sits near the teacher to attentively receive secret/sacred knowledge. Upanishads are also called Vedanta (given at the end of each Veda). Largest Upanishad: Brihadaranyaka (part of Shukla Yajurveda). Satyameva Jayate comes from: Mundaka Upanishad (part of Atharvaveda). Dialogue between Nachiketa and Yama: Katha Upanishad. Four Ashramas mentioned in: Jabala Upanishad.
Q3. Which of the following objectives emphasizes ethical living in the Vedic framework?
A
Artha
B
Kama
C
Dharma ✔
D
Moksha
✅ Answer: C — Dharma (ethical/righteous living). The four Purusharthas: Dharma (righteousness/duty), Artha (materialism/wealth — not forbidden), Kama (desires/pleasures), Moksha (liberation/giving up everything). Dharma comes first because ethical living is the foundation.
Q4. What term was used to describe the student stage of life in early Hindu philosophy?
A
Vanaprastha
B
Brahmacharya ✔
C
Sanyasin
D
Grihastha
✅ Answer: B — Brahmacharya (student life, up to age 25). The Four Ashramas: Brahmacharya (0–25, student), Grihastha (25–50, householder), Vanaprastha (50–75, retired/forest-dweller), Sannyasa (75+, renunciation). Written in the Jabala Upanishad.
Q5. What was the role of the Adhvaryu priest according to the Yajurveda?
A
Composed new ragas
B
Performed dance rituals
C
Conducted yagnas and chanted mantras in low tones ✔
D
Wrote scriptures based on music theory
✅ Answer: C — The Adhvaryu conducted yagnas and chanted mantras in low tones. Yajurveda tells us about prayers and rituals. It is divided into: Shukla Yajurveda (pure/white) and Krishna Yajurveda (mixed/black). Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (largest) belongs to Shukla Yajurveda. Atharvaveda contains magical spells and charms.
Q6. Which Veda is primarily known for its musical content?
A
Rigveda
B
Samaveda ✔
C
Yajurveda
D
Atharvaveda
✅ Answer: B — Samaveda is the oldest book on music. Rigveda: oldest Veda, 1028 hymns, 10 Mandalas (3rd = Gayatri Mantra, 7th = Battle of Ten Kings on Purushani/Ravi River, 9th = Lord Soma/God of Plants, 10th = Purusha Sukta hymn on caste origin). Compiled in Early Vedic Age (1500–1000 BC). Yajurveda = prayers/rituals. Atharvaveda = magical spells/charms.

Remember the Four Vedas — "RiSaYaA": Rigveda (oldest, hymns) → Samaveda (music/songs) → Yajurveda (rituals) → Atharvaveda (magic spells)

FOUR ASHRAMAS (from Jabala Upanishad)
  • Brahmacharya (0–25 yrs) — Student life, celibacy, learning
  • Grihastha (25–50 yrs) — Householder, family, earning
  • Vanaprastha (50–75 yrs) — Retired, forest-dwelling, detachment
  • Sannyasa (75+ yrs) — Complete renunciation, seeking moksha
PURUSHARTHA (Four Aims of Life)
Dharma (duty/ethics) → Artha (wealth/prosperity) → Kama (desires/pleasure) → Moksha (liberation). Dharma is the foundation — earn wealth ethically, enjoy pleasures righteously, then attain liberation.

🏛️ Section 2 — Mauryan Empire & Magadha
Q7. Ashoka's Dhamma was primarily aimed at —
A
Expanding Buddhism as the state religion
B
Promoting administrative centralization
C
Encouraging ethical living and social harmony ✔
D
Establishing alliances with foreign rulers
✅ Answer: C — Encouraging ethical living and social harmony. Ashoka's Dhamma was propagated through rock edicts and pillar edicts. Major Rock Edict 3 reveals the Dhamma policy. Major Rock Edict 4: Dhammaghosh replaced Beryghosh. Major Rock Edict 8: Dhamma Yatra ceremonies. Rock Edict 13: Kalinga War (fought 8 years after coronation). His actual name "Devanampriya Piyadasi Ashoka" found in minor rock edicts at Maski, Nettur, Brahmagiri (Andhra Pradesh) and Gurjara (MP).
Q8. Which event is considered the turning point in Ashoka's life, leading him to embrace Dhamma?
A
His coronation ceremony
B
The Kalinga War ✔
C
Meeting with Buddhist monks
D
Death of his father Bindusara
✅ Answer: B — Kalinga War (261 BC), fought 8 years after coronation. Ashoka took the throne with help of Radha Gupta (officer in Bindusara's court). He murdered his brother Sushima. After witnessing the destruction of Kalinga War, he embraced Buddhism. He sent his son Mahendra and daughter Sanghamitra to Sri Lanka (then Ceylon) to spread Buddhism.
Q9. Who succeeded Bimbisara after allegedly killing and forcing him to commit suicide?
A
Ashoka
B
Bindusara
C
Ajatashatru ✔
D
Chandragupta Maurya
✅ Answer: C — Ajatashatru (meaning "enemy of his own clan"). He imprisoned/killed his father Bimbisara to seize power. After Ajatashatru came Udayin, who shifted the capital from Rajgriha to Pataliputra. Bimbisara had sent his physician Jivaka to cure Pradyota (king of Avanti/Ujjain).
Q10. Which dynasty came after the Shishunagar dynasty in Magadha?
A
Gupta
B
Nanda ✔
C
Maurya
D
Haryanka
✅ Answer: B — Nanda Dynasty. Magadha dynasty succession: Haryanka → Shishunaga → Nanda → Maurya. Haryanka founder: Bimbisara. Shishunaga's famous ruler: Kalashoka (patronized the 2nd Buddhist Council, 383 BC). Nanda founder: Mahapadmananda ("uprooter of all Kshatriyas"). Last Nanda ruler: Dhanananda (during Alexander's invasion). Alexander defeated Porus at Battle of Hydaspes (on Jhelum River). Later, Chandragupta Maurya defeated Seleucus Nicator and married his daughter Helena. Seleucus sent Megasthenes (wrote book "Indica" about Mauryan Empire).
Q11. The Arthashastra, traditionally attributed to Chanakya, mainly deals with —
A
Poetry and literature
B
Religious rituals
C
Statecraft and administration ✔
D
Astronomy
✅ Answer: C — Statecraft and administration. Written by Chanakya/Vishnugupta/Kautilya. Divided into 20 sections. Contains the Saptanga Theory (7 elements of state): Swami (king), Amatya (ministers), Janapada (territory/people), Durga (fort), Kosha (treasury), Danda (army), Mitra (ally).
Q12. Who is known as the Father of Ayurveda?
A
Sushruta
B
Charaka ✔
C
Nagarjuna
D
Vasudeva
✅ Answer: B — Charaka. He edited the Charaka Samhita (originally written by Agnivesh). Sushruta = Father of Surgery. Nagarjuna = "Second Buddha" (founder of Madhyamika school in Mahayana Buddhism). Mahayana Buddhism: considers Buddha divine, found in China/Korea/Japan. Hinayana: sees Buddha in human form, found in Sri Lanka/Southeast Asia.

Magadha Dynasty Order — "HaShi NaMa Gup": Haryanka → Shishunaga → Nanda → Maurya → Gupta (later period)

Ashoka's Edicts — Key Facts for Exams: Major Rock Edicts: 14 total. Rock Edict 3 = Dhamma policy. Rock Edict 13 = Kalinga War. Minor rock edicts at Maski, Nettur, Brahmagiri (Andhra Pradesh) & Gurjara (MP) reveal his real name: Devanampriya Piyadasi Ashoka.

Don't confuse: Charaka = Father of Ayurveda | Sushruta = Father of Surgery | Nagarjuna = "Second Buddha" (Madhyamika school founder)

👑 Section 3 — Gupta Empire & Golden Age
Q13. What is the name of the Licchavi princess married to Chandragupta I?
A
Kadamba Devi
B
Nayanika
C
Kumar Devi ✔
D
Prabhavati
✅ Answer: C — Kumar Devi. Chandragupta I (319–335 AD) assumed the title Maharajadhiraja. This Licchavi marriage alliance strengthened Gupta power. Note: Prabhavati Gupta was Chandragupta II's daughter (married to Vakataka ruler Rudrasena II).
Q14. Who succeeded Chandragupta I as the ruler of the Gupta Empire?
A
Chandragupta II
B
Samudragupta ✔
C
Skandagupta
D
Kumaragupta
✅ Answer: B — Samudragupta (335–380 AD), called the "Napoleon of India." He is depicted playing the Veena on coins. The Prayag Prashasti was composed for him by his court poet Harisena. After him came Chandragupta II (380+ AD) who took the title Vikramaditya.
Q15. Which of the following is NOT a work by Kalidasa?
A
Meghadutam
B
Kumar Shambhava
C
Mricchakatikam ✔
D
Abhigyan Shakuntalam
✅ Answer: C — Mricchakatikam ("The Little Clay Cart") was written by Shudraka, not Kalidasa. Kalidasa (Shakespeare of India) was a Navaratna in Chandragupta II's court. Other Kalidasa works: Malavikagnimitram (love story of Malavika and Agnimitra, son of Pushyamitra Shunga).
Q16. In whose court is the celebrated Sanskrit poet Kalidasa believed to have flourished?
A
Skandagupta
B
Samudragupta
C
Chandragupta II ✔
D
Kumaragupta
✅ Answer: C — Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya). The Navaratnas in his court: Kalidasa (Sanskrit poet), Harisena (poet, composed Prayag Prashasti), Amarsimha (lexicographer), Dhanvantari (physician), Kahapanaka (astrologer), Sanku (architect), Varahamihira (astronomer), Vararuchi (grammarian), Vetala Bhatta (magician). Kumaragupta built Nalanda University. Vikramshila was built by Dharmapala. Udantpuri was built by Gopala (Pala dynasty founder).
Q17. Who was considered the most notable of the Nine Luminaries in Chandragupta II's court?
A
Varahamihira
B
Kalidasa ✔
C
Dhanvantari
D
Amarsimha
✅ Answer: B — Kalidasa. Known as the "Shakespeare of India." Banabhatta wrote Harshavardhana's biography "Harsha Charita" and the novel "Kadambari." Harshavardhana himself wrote three books: Ratnavali, Priyadarshika, and Nagananda.

CHANDRAGUPTA II — KEY FACTS
  • Two wives: Dhruva Devi (1st, originally wife of brother Ramgupta) & Kubera Naga (Naga princess)

  • Story told in: Devi Chandraguptam by Vishakhadatta (who also wrote Mudra Rakshasa)
  • Mudra Rakshasa reveals Mauryas belonged to lower clans

Navaratnas of Chandragupta II: "KHA-D-K-S-V-V-V-A-H" — Kalidasa, Harisena, Amarsimha, Dhanvantari, Kahapanaka, Sanku, Varahamihira, Vararuchi, Vetala Bhatta

⛵ Section 4 — Chola Dynasty & Administration
Q18. In which year did Raja Raja I become the king of the Chola Empire?
A
1001 AD
B
985 AD ✔
C
1070 AD
D
947 AD
✅ Answer: B — 985 AD. Raja Raja I ruled 985–1014 AD. Chola dynasty belonged to the Sangam Age. Two phases: Early Chola and Later/Imperial Chola (founded by Vijayalaya Chola). Capital: Thanjavur or Uraiyur. Raja Raja I built the Brihadeshwara Temple (also called Rajarajeshwara Temple) at Thanjavur. Rajendra Chola I built another Brihadeshwara Temple at Gangaikondacholapuram. Both are UNESCO World Heritage Sites (1987). Rajendra Chola I = "Napoleon of South India." He conquered northern Sri Lanka (Anuradhapura), annexed the Sri Vijaya Empire (Shailendra Dynasty), and crossed the Ganges taking the title "Gangaikonda Chola."
Q19. Which of the following pairs is correct regarding the land categories mentioned in Chola inscriptions?
A
Shalabhoga — for Brahmins
B
Devadana — for temples ✔
C
Brahmadeya — for Jain monks
D
Pallichchhandam — for schools
✅ Answer: B — Devadana = land for temples. Shalabhoga = land for schools. Brahmadeya = land for Brahmins. Pallichchhandam = land for Jain monks/institutions.
Q20. What main role did the Cauvery River channels play in Chola period agriculture?
A
They provided fertile soil and moisture for crops ✔
B
They provided protection from invasions
C
They transported goods for trade
D
They were used for fishing and boat transportation
✅ Answer: A — Cauvery delta is called the "Garden of South India." The Cauvery River is perennial (receives both SW & NE monsoon rainfall). Originates in Brahmagiri Ranges, Kodagu district, Karnataka (place: Talakaveri). Tributaries (have 'ni'/'ti' endings): Bhavani, Kabini, Hemavati, Amaravati, Arkavati. Mekedatu Dam project: where Kaveri meets Arkavati (Karnataka-TN dispute). Vijayalaya Chola defeated Mutrayar in the Cauvery Delta and built a temple for Goddess Nishumbasudani.
CHOLA LAND CLASSIFICATION
  • Vellanvagai: Land of non-Brahmin peasant proprietors
  • Brahmadeya: Land gifted to Brahmins
  • Shalabhoga: Land for maintenance of schools
  • Devadana: Land gifted to temples
  • Pallichchhandam: Land donated to Jain institutions

Chola Naval Power: Cholas were famous for their efficient naval system. Rajendra Chola I conquered the Sri Vijaya Empire (modern Malaysia/Indonesia) and Ceylon (northern Sri Lanka — Anuradhapura).

📜 Section 5 — Sangam Age & Tamil Literature
Q21. In the context of the Sangam era, all three Sangams were held under the patronage of —
A
Gupta Kings
B
Pandya Kings ✔
C
Maurya Kings
D
Satavahana Kings
✅ Answer: B — Pandya Kings. Sangam period: 3rd century BC to 3rd century AD. Three Sangams: 1st and 3rd held at Madurai (Pandya capital), 2nd at Kapadapuram. The 2nd Sangam was presided over by Tolkappiyar who composed "Tolkappiyam" (oldest Tamil text). Other texts: Silappadikaram by Ilango Adigal (triangular love story), Manimekalai by Sattanar.
Q22. Which city served as the capital of the Pandya Kings?
A
Ujjain
B
Vaishali
C
Amaravati
D
Madurai ✔
✅ Answer: D — Madurai. Madurai was famous for pearls (mentioned in Megasthenes' book "Indica"). Ujjain = capital of Avanti (Mahajanapada). Vaishali = capital of Vajji. Amaravati = new capital of Andhra Pradesh. Pandya emblem: Fish. Port: Korkai.
Q23. Which important chieftains dominated the Tamil region during the Sangam Age?

Chalukya, Chera, Maurya

Chera, Chola, Pandya ✔
C
Maurya, Chola, Pandya

Satavahana dynasty: founder Simuka, greatest ruler Gautamiputra Satakarni, capital Paithan/Pratishthan. They introduced lead/potin coins and were first to make land donations to Brahmins. Nasik Inscription composed by Gautami Balasri. Matrilineal society. Which Chera ruler started the Pattini Cult? Senguttuvan.

⚔️ Section 6 — Post-Gupta & Regional Kingdoms
Q24. Which regional power rose to prominence in Northern India after the decline of the Gupta Empire?
A
Cholas
B
Vakatakas
C
Pushyabhutis ✔
D
Cheras
✅ Answer: C — Pushyabhuti Dynasty (also called Vardhana Dynasty). Harshavardhana belonged to this dynasty. Initial capital: Thanesar (Haryana). Father: Prabhakarvardhan. Sons: Harshavardhana & Rajyavardhan. Sister: Rajyashri (married to Grihavarman of Maukhari dynasty, capital Kannauj). Harsha made Kannauj his capital. Rule: 606–647 AD. Defeated by Pulakeshin II at Battle of Narmada (618 AD). After Harsha's death: Tripartite Struggle for Kannauj (Palas vs Rashtrakutas vs Gurjar Pratiharas, 9th–11th century AD).
Q25. Rudrasena II was a ruler belonging to which dynasty?
A
Nanda
B
Maurya
C
Vakataka ✔
D
Gurjar Pratihara
✅ Answer: C — Vakataka Dynasty (Maharashtra/Deccan region). Rudrasena II married Prabhavati Gupta (Chandragupta II's daughter). Vakataka founder: Vindhyashakti (V for Vakataka, V for Vindhyashakti). Capitals: Nandivardhana, Pravarapura (not fixed). Rule: 3rd–6th century AD. Built the Ajanta Caves (29+1 caves, all Buddhist). Key ruler for Ajanta: Harisena. Note: This is NOT the same Harisena who wrote Prayag Prashasti for Samudragupta. Gupta-Vakataka alliance removed Shakas from Gujarat.

A
Rajasthan
B
Gujarat ✔
C
Maharashtra
D
Madhya Pradesh
✅ Answer: B — Gujarat. The Maitraka Dynasty ruled Vallabhi in Gujarat. Harsha's daughter was married to Dhruvasena II (Maitraka ruler).
Q27. Gurjar Pratihara Harichandra is known for establishing a kingdom in which region?
A
Tamil Nadu
B
Rajasthan ✔

D

Q28. Which of the following queens changed her name on inscriptions and pretended to be a man?
A
Rani Durgavati
B
Rudrama Devi ✔
C
Chand Bibi
D
Durgavati Devi
✅ Answer: B — Rudrama Devi of the Kakatiya Dynasty (capital: Warangal). Changed her name to "Rudradeva" because it was male-dominated society. Her father had no sons, so he appointed her as successor. Kakatiyas initially built Golconda Fort (where Kohinoor diamond was mined). Malik Kafur (Alauddin Khilji's commander, "Hazar Dinari") defeated: Yadavas of Devagiri → Hoysalas of Dwarasamudra → Kakatiyas of Warangal → Pandyas of Madurai. Chand Bibi: Regent of Bijapur Sultanate (Adil Shahi Dynasty) for Ibrahim Adil Shah II. Durgavati Devi (Durga Bhabhi): wife of Bhagwati Charan Vohra (wrote "Philosophy of Bomb"), helped Bhagat Singh escape by posing as his wife.
Q29. The Chalukyas of Badami are noted for which contribution to Indian art?
A
Bronze sculpture of Nataraja
B
Rock-cut cave temples at Badami ✔
C
Development of Nagara style temple
D
Paintings at Ellora Caves
✅ Answer: B — Rock-cut cave temples at Badami. Chalukyas developed Vesara style (Nagara + Dravidian). Temples: Badami caves, Aihole (Cradle of Temple Architecture — Ladkhan, Durga, Ravanapadi, Galaganatha temples), Pattadakal (Virupaksha Temple by Queen Lokamahadevi). Two Virupaksha temples: one in Hampi (by Lakundi/Hakka-Bukka), one in Pattadakal. Bronze Nataraja = Chola Empire (lost-wax technique). Ellora: 34 caves (Hindu + Jain + Buddhist), Cave 16 = Kailash Temple (largest monolithic, by Krishna I of Rashtrakutas). Nagara style = Guptas. Dravidian style = Pallavas.

Chalukya capitals: Badami/Vatapi (Early), Kalyani (Later), Vengi (Eastern). Narasimha Varman I completed the Pancharatha at Mahabalipuram. Narasimha Varman II (Rajasimha) built the Shore Temple at Mahabalipuram.

☸️ Section 7 — Buddhism, Jainism & Kushan Dynasty
Q33. Kanishka is famous in history as a great patron of —
A
Jainism
B
Buddhism ✔
C
Christianity
D
Zoroastrianism
✅ Answer: B — Buddhism. Kanishka belonged to the Kushan Dynasty. First capital: Purushapura (modern Peshawar), later Mathura. Developed the Mathura School of Art (indigenous, red sandstone). Gandhara School = Indo-Greek art (grey schist stone, Greek influence). Kanishka = greatest Kushan ruler. Purest gold coins minted during his reign. Patronized the 4th Buddhist Council at Kundalvan, Kashmir. He is considered the 3rd or 4th ruler (Rabataka Inscription in Afghanistan says 4th).
Q34. The emergence of Buddhism and Jainism in the 6th century BC can be linked to —
A
Foreign invasions
B
Economic prosperity
C
Criticism of Vedic rituals and caste rigidity ✔
D
Natural disasters
✅ Answer: C — Criticism of Vedic rituals and caste rigidity. In the Later Vedic Age, caste became rigid and women's status declined. Vedic rituals gave Brahmins a sense of superiority. Lower castes needed an alternative religion where all were equal. Both Buddhism and Jainism preached in local languages (Pali & Prakrit) instead of Sanskrit, making them accessible to common people.

A
Mahabharata
B
Ramayana ✔
C
Bhagavata Purana
D
Vishnu Purana
✅ Answer: B — Ramayana. Dussehra celebrates Lord Rama's victory over Ravana.
Q36. Which language was adopted by Jainism as its sacred language in ancient India?
A
Sanskrit
B
Prakrit ✔
C
Pali

Q37. Which Harappan site is located in Jammu and Kashmir?
A
Kalibangan
B
Manda ✔
C

✅ Answer: B — Manda (J&K). Harappan extent: Manda (J&K, north) to Daimabad (Maharashtra, south), Alamgirpur (UP, east) to Sutkagen-dor (Pakistan-Iran border, west). Key sites: Kalibangan (Rajasthan, Ghaggar River — fire altars, plowed fields, black bangles). Banawali (Haryana — first barley evidence, no grid pattern, terracotta plow model). Lothal (Gujarat — ancient dockyard, double/joint burial, fire altars, discovered by SR Rao). Ropar (Punjab, Sutlej River — pit burial). Chanhudaro (Sindh — city without citadel, on Indus River). Dholavira (Gujarat — divided into 3 parts, Jand water reservoir).

Lothal

✅ Answer: C — Mohenjo-daro (Sindh, Pakistan). The Dancing Girl: bronze, Tribhanga posture, lost-wax technique, 10.5 cm / 4 inches, now in National Museum, Delhi. Harappa is in Punjab province (Pakistan).
