① Epics & Geography — Uttarayan 🌞
Q1. In which epic is it mentioned that dying during Uttarayan leads to salvation (moksha)?
A
Ramayana
B
Mahabharata ✔
C
Rigveda
D
Puranas
✅ Answer: B — Mahabharata (written by Ved Vyas). Dying during Uttarayan is said to lead to salvation.

What is Uttarayan? The northward movement of the Sun.

  • The Equator divides Earth into Northern & Southern Hemispheres.
  • Sun moves between Tropic of Cancer (23½° N) and Tropic of Capricorn (23½° S) — this belt = Torrid Zone (gets direct/overhead rays → very hot).
  • Frigid Zone (66½°) → 6 months day, 6 months night.
  • June 21 = Sun on Tropic of Cancer (its max limit) = International Yoga Day; after this Dakshinayan begins.
  • December 22 = Sun reaches Tropic of Capricorn = World Meditation Day.
  • Makar Sankranti (January) celebrates the start of Uttarayan. Uttarayan phase ≈ January to end-June / mid-July.

✅ Answer: B — Criticism of Vedic rituals/sacrifices and growing caste discrimination.
Increasing Vedic ritual sacrifices & caste rigidity gave Brahmins superiority → lower castes needed an alternative religion offering equality.
In the Early Vedic Age caste rigidity was low & women's status good; in the Later Vedic Age (Iron Age) caste rigidity increased & women's status declined.

㊲ Current/Static — Infant Mortality Rate 📉
Q37. The decline of the Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) in India can be primarily attributed to —
A
Better neonatal care
B
Expanded immunization (e.g., Mission Indradhanush)
C
Improved maternal health
D
All of the above ✔
✅ Answer: D — All of the above. IMR = infant deaths per 1,000 live births; in 2011 ≈ 44. Decline due to better neonatal care, expanded immunization (e.g., Mission Indradhanush) and improved maternal health.

㊳ Mauryas — Turning Point in Ashoka's Life ☸️
Q38. Which event is considered the turning point in Ashoka's life, leading him to embrace Dhamma?
A
His coronation
B
The Kalinga War ✔
C
The 3rd Buddhist Council
D
Death of Bindusara
✅ Answer: B — The Kalinga War (261 BC), fought 8 years after his coronation.
Ashoka ascended the throne with help of Radhagupta (an officer of his father Bindusara); killed his brother Sushima.
Later sent his son Mahendra & daughter Sanghamitra to Sri Lanka (Ceylon) to spread Buddhism.

㊵ Religion — Language of Jainism 🗣️
Q40. Which language was adopted by Jainism as its sacred/secret language in ancient India?
A
Sanskrit
B
Prakrit ✔
C
Pali
D
Tamil
✅ Answer: B — Prakrit. Buddhism & Jainism flourished by using local languages (Pali & Prakrit) instead of Sanskrit — Buddhist texts mainly in Pali, Jain texts mainly in Prakrit.

Tripitaka (Pali Buddhist canon):

  • Vinaya Pitaka — rules & codes for monks and nuns
  • Sutta Pitaka — Buddha's sermons
  • Abhidhamma Pitaka — philosophical teachings
