⑤ Economics — Elasticity, Utility & Taxation 💰
Q5. Total revenue is maximized when price elasticity of demand equals what value?
A
Elasticity = 1 (Unitary elastic) ✔
✅ Answer: Revenue is maximized when Elasticity = 1 — demand changes proportionally to price change

Elasticity Types:

  • Price Elasticity = % Change in Demand ÷ % Change in Price
  • Income Elasticity = % Change in Demand ÷ % Change in Income

Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility: Utility (satisfaction) from each additional unit consumed decreases. Sum of all marginal utilities = Total Utility.

Q6. Which best explains why indirect taxes are considered regressive?
A
They apply equally to all income groups, burdening the poor proportionally more ✔
✅ Answer: Indirect taxes (e.g., GST) apply the same rate to everyone regardless of income — this hits lower-income groups harder proportionally
Tax TypeNatureExamples
Direct TaxProgressive — increases with incomeIncome tax, Wealth tax, Gift tax ("paper taxes" — low collection)
Indirect TaxRegressive — same rate for allGST, Service tax, Sales tax, Excise, Customs

Only ~3% of India's population pays direct taxes — but everyone pays indirect taxes (e.g., GST on purchases) even if they don't realize it, since sellers deposit it on their behalf.

Surcharge vs Cess — Kept ENTIRELY by Centre (not shared with states):

  • Surcharge — "tax on tax" for high-income earners
  • Cess — levied for a specific purpose (e.g., education cess, pollution cess) — applies to everyone

Laffer Curve: As tax rate ↑, tax collection ↑ — but after a point, collection starts to decline (bell-shaped curve)

⑥ Modern History — Revolt of 1857 🚩
Q7. Assertion (A): Pir Ali Khan was executed during the 1857 Revolt. Reason (R): He was a British informant who switched sides during the uprising.
A
A is true, but R is false ✔
B
Both A and R are true
C
Both are false
D
A is false, R is true
✅ Answer: A — Pir Ali Khan was indeed hanged during 1857, BUT he was NOT a British informant — he actually started a rebellion AGAINST the British and remained loyal to the cause throughout
1857 Revolt — Causes & Timeline
Background Governor-General: Canning (later became the first Viceroy under the Govt. of India Act 1858 / Queen's Proclamation)
Policies causing anger Doctrine of Lapse (Awadh annexed in 1856, last Nawab Wajid Ali Shah); General Service Enlistment Act (forced overseas service — against religious beliefs)
Immediate cause Greased cartridges — Mangal Pandey (34th Native Infantry) refused to use them, was hanged; revolt began April 8, 1857
May 10, 1857 Meerut soldiers refused to fire; freed jailed comrades → started the "Delhi Chalo" campaign

Why the 1857 Revolt Failed:

  • Did not reach South India
  • No single unifying national idea — everyone fought for their own local cause
  • Lack of full-fledged mass movement/participation

⑬ Geography — Southern Oscillation, El Niño & ITCZ 🌊
Q14. Which of the following correctly defines the Southern Oscillation?
A
Periodic changes in pressure conditions in the Pacific & Indian Ocean ✔
B
Periodic pressure changes across India due to ITCZ shifts
C
Periodic reversal of winds in the Indian Peninsula (Monsoon)
D
Reversal of jet streams during the monsoon season
✅ Answer: A — Southern Oscillation = periodic pressure changes in the Pacific and Indian Ocean — linked to El Niño

ITCZ (Intertropical Convergence Zone):

  • Zone where NE & SE trade winds converge near the equator
  • NOT fixed at 0° — it's a variable belt, shifting with the sun's apparent movement
  • June 21 (Summer Solstice) — sun overhead at Tropic of Cancer → ITCZ shifts north
  • Doldrums — belt near equator (5°N–5°S), calm winds
  • Horse Latitudes — belt at 30°N–30°S
El Niño vs La Niña
PhenomenonMeaningEffect
El Niño"Little Boy" (Christ Child) — trade winds weakenWarm water stays in E. Pacific → NO upwelling → fishing industry (Peru) hurt; poor monsoon in India; drought in Australia
La Niña"Little Girl" — opposite of El NiñoTrade winds strengthen; heavy rainfall in Australia AND India

Related Oscillations:

  • Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD) — Positive IOD (low pressure west, high pressure east of Indian Ocean) = good rainfall in India
  • Madden-Julian Oscillation (MJO) — cluster of clouds/rain moving from Indian Ocean → Pacific Ocean, affects monsoon intensity
🧠 Trade Winds: NE Trades & SE Trades are Primary/Permanent winds (along with Westerlies and Polar Easterlies). Jet Streams = high-speed upper atmosphere winds; when Tropical Easterly Jet Streams weaken/shift (replaced by Westerly Jet Streams), monsoon in India weakens.

Q19. Wood's Dispatch of 1854 recommended several changes to the Indian education system. Which was a key recommendation?
A
Promote vernacular languages at lower levels; English only at higher levels ✔
✅ Answer: Wood's Dispatch (1854), under Charles Wood — called the "Magna Carta of Indian Education" — promoted vernacular languages at LOWER levels, English only at HIGHER education level. Introduced during Dalhousie's tenure

Macaulay's Minute (1835) vs Wood's Dispatch (1854):

  • Macaulay's Minute (1835) — under William Bentinck; promoted English education at ALL levels; proposed the "Downward Filtration Theory" (educate elites in English → filters down to masses)
  • Wood's Dispatch (1854) — under Charles Wood; REJECTED downward filtration theory; promoted vernacular at lower levels

⑲ Geography/Science — Moon's Atmosphere 🌕
Q20. Which of the following best explains why the Moon lacks a significant atmosphere?
A
Lack of gravity ✔
B
Strong solar winds
C
Excessive volcanism
D
Rapid rotation
✅ Answer: A — Lack of gravity — Moon's gravity is only 1/6th of Earth's, insufficient to hold an atmosphere in place

Moon — Key Facts:

  • Highest point on Moon: Leibnitz Mountains
  • Rotation time = Revolution time = 27 days (synchronous rotation) — hence only one side (~60%) is ever visible from Earth; hidden ~40% = "Far Side of the Moon"
  • Perigee = Moon's closest point to Earth; Apogee = farthest point

Atmospheric Evolution (Earth) — 3 Stages: 1) Loss of primordial H/He atmosphere (via solar winds/flares) 2) Volcanic degassing 3) Modification by the living world (photosynthesis, etc.)

✅ Answer: A — Rising general price level (inflation) = falling value/purchasing power of money

Inflation Control — Fiscal vs Monetary Policy:

  • Monetary Policy (RBI/Central Bank) — contractionary: ↑CRR, ↑SLR, ↑Repo rate, ↑Bank rate, sell govt securities
  • Fiscal Policy (Government) — contractionary: ↑Income tax, ↓Subsidies, ↓MSP
  • Demand-pull inflation — excess money/demand chasing few goods
  • Cost-push (Supply-side) inflation — includes Bottleneck inflation (e.g., Strait of Hormuz disruption → costlier oil/transport → cascades to other goods)

💡 Topper Tips — Blitz Lecture 15 Ke Liye Yaad Rakho!
  • Goru Bihu = cattle worship (Day 1 of Rangali Bihu) — NOT sky lamps! Read festival names carefully
  • Art. 323A/323B = Tribunals, added by 42nd Amendment — often confused with Elections/UT/States parts
  • 1919 Act = Dyarchy at Provinces; 1935 Act = Dyarchy at Centre (opposite!) — a classic trap
  • Indirect tax = Regressive (same rate for all); Direct tax = Progressive (rises with income)
  • Bhakra Nangal (largest/highest gravity dam) ≠ Tehri (highest dam overall) ≠ Hirakud (longest dam) — three different superlatives!
  • GDP + NFIA = GNP — remember Net Factor Income from Abroad
  • Wood's Dispatch (1854) REJECTED Macaulay's (1835) Downward Filtration Theory — promoted vernacular at lower levels
  • El Niño = weak monsoon/drought trigger; La Niña = heavy rainfall — opposite effects!
