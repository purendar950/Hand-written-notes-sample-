① Art & Culture — Rajasthan Folk Dance 💃
Q1. Which folk dance of Rajasthan was originally performed by the Bhil tribe and is now a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage?
A
Kalbelia
B
Chari Dance
C
Ghoomar ✔
D
Terah Taali
✅ Answer: C — Ghoomar | Originally Bhil tribe's dance | Women spin in ghaghra | UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage
Major Folk Dances of Rajasthan
DanceKey Features
GhoomarWomen spin in circles wearing ghaghra | Originally by Bhil tribe | Performed at all auspicious occasions | UNESCO Intangible Heritage
KalbeliaDance of Kalbelia (snake charmer) community | Sinuous, serpentine moves | Black dress | UNESCO 2010
Chari DanceWomen balance brass pots (chari) with fire on head | Kishangarh region
Terah TaaliWomen sit & play 13 manjiras (cymbals) tied to body | Dedicated to Baba Ramdev
BhavaiWomen balance earthen pots on head while dancing on glass or swords

Rajasthan Trick — G-K-C-T:

Ghoomar = Ghoom (spin) | Kalbelia = Kal/snake | Chari = Container on head | Terah Taali = 13 cymbals

㉒ Art & Culture — Folk vs Classical Distinction 🎭
Q22. Which of the following statements about Folk Dance is INCORRECT?
A
Folk dances are performed by common people on special occasions
B
Folk dances are related to seasons, harvests, festivals and rituals
C
Folk dances require formal training under a guru in a gurukul system ✔
D
Folk dances vary from region to region across India
✅ Answer: C — Formal training in gurukul system is a feature of CLASSICAL dance (not folk) | Folk dance is spontaneous, community-based, no formal training needed
FOLK DANCE
→ Performed by common people
→ NO formal training needed
→ Related to seasons, harvests, festivals
→ Spontaneous, community-based
→ Varies region to region
CLASSICAL DANCE (8 forms)
→ Based on Natyashastra (Bharata Muni)
→ Formal training + Gurukul system
→ Recognised by Sangeet Natak Akademi
→ Bharatanatyam · Kathak · Odissi
→ Kuchipudi · Manipuri · Sattriya · Mohiniyattam · Kathakali
