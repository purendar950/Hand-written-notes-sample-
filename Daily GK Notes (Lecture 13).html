Q1. What are oligosaccharides?
A
Carbohydrates that yield 1 monosaccharide unit on hydrolysis
B
Carbohydrates that yield more than 10 monosaccharide units on hydrolysis
C
Carbohydrates that yield 2 to 10 monosaccharide units on hydrolysis ✔
D
Carbohydrates that do not yield any monosaccharide units
✅ Answer: C — yield 2 to 10 monosaccharide units.
CARBOHYDRATES BY HYDROLYSIS
  • Monosaccharide → yields 1 unit (e.g., glucose, fructose).
  • Oligosaccharide → yields 2 to 10 units.
  • Polysaccharide → yields more than 10 units (e.g., starch, cellulose).

🏔️ Physical Geography — Geomorphology & the Himalayas
Q6. How does India's Peninsular Plateau differ from the Himalayan region in terms of geological stability?
✓
Peninsular Plateau is ancient & stable; the Himalayas are young & still changing ✔
✅ The Peninsular Plateau is old & stable (few earthquakes). The Himalayas are young & actively changing (earthquake- & landslide-prone).
FORCES THAT SHAPE THE EARTH

Two categories of geomorphic forces:

  • Endogenic (internal) → plate tectonics. Collective term = Diastrophism.
  • Exogenic (external) → weathering, erosion, mass movement. Collective term = Denudation; primary energy comes from the Sun.
EXOGENIC — DRIVING FORCES
Weathering → driven by molecular stresses · Erosion → driven by kinetic energy · Mass movement → driven by gravity.
⚠ Weathering is NOT a prerequisite for mass movement — gravity is the real driver of mass movement.
Plate Tectonics — who gave what
Theory / IdeaGiven byYear / Note
Continental Drift TheoryAlfred Wegener1912
Plate Tectonics TheoryMcKenzie & Parker (also Morgan)Morgan mainly explained it
Convection Current Theory (why plates move)Arthur Holmes1930
Sea Floor Spreading TheoryHarry Hess—
Don't mix them up! The plate tectonics theory is credited to McKenzie & Parker / Morgan — NOT Arthur Holmes. Arthur Holmes gave the convection current idea (why plates move).
TYPES OF PLATE MOVEMENT
  • Convergent → destructive boundary.
  • Divergent → constructive boundary.
  • Transform → conservative (neither created nor destroyed).
DIASTROPHISM (ENDOGENIC) — BREAKDOWN
  • Orogenic → mountain-building processes (oro = mountain).
  • Epeirogenic → upliftment/changes over very large parts of the crust.
  • Plus volcanism and earthquakes.
WHY THE HIMALAYAS ARE EARTHQUAKE-PRONE
The Himalayas sit on the Indian Plate – Eurasian Plate convergent boundary, which is still active — the two plates keep moving closer, so the Himalayas are still rising. This makes the region prone to earthquakes & landslides. The ancient, stable Peninsular Plateau sees far fewer earthquakes.

TRANS-HIMALAYAS & PEAKS
  • Trans-Himalayas = Karakoram + Ladakh + Zaskar ranges.
  • Karakoram glaciers: Siachen, Baltoro, Biafo, Hispar. Contains K2 (Godwin Austen) — 8,611 m, world's 2nd-highest & India's highest peak, but in PoK (disputed).
  • Mount Everest (8,848 m) and Kanchenjunga (8,598 m, in Sikkim) are in the Great Himalaya.
DUNS & KAREWAS
Duns = longitudinal valleys between the Lesser Himalaya & Shivalik (e.g., Patli Dun, Kotli Dun, Dehradun — the biggest).
Shivalik disappears in the North-East, replaced by Duars.
Karewas (Kashmir Valley) = lacustrine/glacial deposits — ideal for the local saffron.

🪨 Physical Geography — Rocks & the Deccan Traps
Q7. The Deccan Traps region of India is primarily made up of which type of rock?
✓
Basaltic (igneous) rock — flood basalt ✔
✅ Answer: Basaltic rock (a flood-basalt province). The study of rocks = Petrology.
THE THREE ROCK TYPES
  • Igneous (primary) → from solidification of magma/lava. E.g., granite, basalt, gabbro, pumice.
  • Metamorphic → from recrystallisation of existing rock under heat/pressure (PVT change). Shows foliation/lineation/banding.
  • Sedimentary → eroded sediments deposited layer-by-layer. Fossils are found here. Types: organically, chemically & mechanically (clastic) formed.
Common metamorphic transformations
Parent rockBecomes (metamorphic)
LimestoneMarble
SandstoneQuartzite
GraniteGneiss
ShaleSchist / Slate
DECCAN TRAPS & HOTSPOT VOLCANISM
  • Deccan Traps = flood basalt province; gives black (regur) soil — self-plowing & impermeable, typical of volcanic regions.
  • Formed by the Réunion hotspot. A hotspot volcano forms in the middle of a plate (not at a boundary); lava is believed to come straight from the outer core. E.g., Mauna Loa & Mauna Kea (shield volcanoes).
  • India's only volcanoes: Barren Island & Narcondam (Andaman).
VOLCANO TYPES & CRUST
By lava fluidity: Caldera (explosive) → Composite (thick) → Shield (less thick) → Flood basalt (most fluid).
Crust: Continental = granitic (thick & light); Oceanic = basaltic (thin but denser).
Igneous can be intrusive/plutonic (cools inside, e.g., granite) or extrusive (cools at surface, e.g., basalt). Dynamic metamorphism = metamorphism with no appreciable chemical change.

PLANT ADAPTATIONS (-PHYTES)
  • Xerophytes → adapted to dry conditions (thorn-forest plants).
  • Heliophytes → sun-loving plants.
  • Halophytes → salt-loving / salt-tolerant (mangroves, Sundarbans). "Halo" = salinity.
  • Neophytes → non-native, introduced species (e.g., tobacco, potato — brought by the Portuguese).
GYMNOSPERM vs ANGIOSPERM
Gymnosperms (conifers) = naked seeds, non-flowering. Angiosperms = flowering plants. Tendu leaves are used to roll bidi.

Q10. Unemployment caused by regular seasonal changes during the year is called?
✓
Seasonal unemployment ✔
✅ Answer: Seasonal unemployment — common in India's agriculture sector (work during sowing/harvesting, idle otherwise).
TYPES OF UNEMPLOYMENT
  • Frictional → while switching from one job to another (a.k.a. search / voluntary unemployment).
  • Structural → a skills gap (your skills don't match market demand).
  • Cyclical → during a recession (jobs lost in downturn, regained in recovery).
  • Seasonal → due to regular seasonal change (agriculture).
  • Disguised → hidden; many work on a field but actual productivity ≈ 0 (agriculture).
  • Educated → has a degree but no employment.

LAND REFORMS & THE 9th SCHEDULE
  • "Land to the Tiller" policy + Land Ceiling Act (1976) — cap on max land owned; surplus distributed.
  • Right to Property removed from Fundamental Rights in 1978 (44th Amendment), moved to Part XII as Article 300A (now a legal/constitutional right). Part XII = finance, property & contracts.
  • 1st Amendment added the 9th Schedule + Articles 31A & 31B (laws here were meant to be immune from judicial review).
  • I.R. Coelho case (2007): laws added to the 9th Schedule after 24 April 1973 (Kesavananda Bharati) CAN be reviewed — the Basic Structure Doctrine came from the Kesavananda Bharati case (1973).
SEBI & SIDBI: SEBI (Securities & Exchange Board of India) — established 1988, statutory status in 1992; regulates securities, mutual funds & stock markets; Chairman Tuhin Kanta Pandey.  |  SIDBI (Small Industries Development Bank of India) — established 1990, HQ in Lucknow.
Bonus — Washington Consensus: a set of free-market economic prescriptions (by the IMF / World Bank), designed in 1989 by economist John Williamson to help developing nations overcome crisis & hyperinflation.

ARTICLE 74 & 75 — KEY POINTS
  • Advice of the CoM was made binding by the 42nd Amendment; the 44th Amendment let the President return advice once, but reconsidered advice is binding.
  • Ministers hold office during the pleasure of the President → individual responsibility to the President; collective responsibility to the Lok Sabha.
  • A person can be a Minister for max 6 months without being a member of either House.
  • Ministers' salaries are determined by Parliament.
PRO-TEM SPEAKER & THE +89 FORMULA
Pro-tem Speaker = a temporary Speaker (usually the senior-most member), appointed by the President, who administers the oath to newly elected Lok Sabha members; the House then elects its permanent Speaker.
Formula: Article (Union) + 89 = Article (State). So Art 77 (Union business) + 89 = Art 166 (State business, in the Governor's name).
Delimitation Commission has been set up 4 times: 1952, 1962, 1972, and 2002.

⚖️ Polity — The Five Writs
Q17. Which writ examines the legality of a person holding a public office?
✓
Quo Warranto ✔
✅ Answer: Quo Warranto ("by what authority") — issued when someone holds a public office they are not eligible for.
The five writs (borrowed from the UK)
WritMeaning & use
Habeas Corpus"to have the body of" — against unlawful detention
Mandamus"we command" — against an official not doing a mandatory duty; not against President/Governor or for discretionary work
CertiorariHigher court quashes a lower court's order (jurisdiction exceeded); can lie against administrative authorities, not legislative
Prohibition"to forbid" — higher court stops a lower court from exceeding its jurisdiction; not against legislative/administrative bodies
Quo Warranto"by what authority" — against unlawful holding of a public office
WRITS — IMPORTANT CASES & ARTICLES
  • Borrowed from the UK (also: Rule of Law, CAG, bicameralism, parliamentary government).
  • ADM Jabalpur v. Shivkant Shukla (1976) = the Habeas Corpus case; Justice H.R. Khanna dissented (right to life is a basic human right).
  • Maneka Gandhi case (1978): wider interpretation; recognised "due process of law" (a US concept). Art 21 says "procedure established by law" — borrowed from Japan.
  • Writs issued by SC under Art 32 (a Fundamental Right — cannot be refused) and HC under Art 226 (not a FR, but wider — enforces fundamental + legal rights).

🏛️ Polity — The Governor
Q18. What is the primary role of the Governor regarding most state universities?
✓
Ex-officio Chancellor — appoints the Vice-Chancellor ✔
✅ The Governor is the ex-officio Chancellor of state universities and appoints the Vice-Chancellor.
GOVERNOR — DISCRETIONARY / KEY POWERS
  • Art 200 → reserve a state Bill for the President's consideration.
  • Art 213 → promulgate ordinances when the state legislature is not in session.
  • Art 161 → pardoning power (cf. Art 72 for the President). But the Governor cannot pardon a death sentence or a court-martial sentence; the President can.

📜 Polity — Constituent Assembly & Preamble
Q19. The Objectives Resolution was moved in the Constituent Assembly by?
✓
Jawaharlal Nehru ✔
·
Patel / Ambedkar / Rajendra Prasad
✅ Answer: Jawaharlal Nehru — moved it on 13 Dec 1946; it became the basis for the Preamble (adopted 22 Jan 1947).
CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY — FACTS
  • Formed per the Cabinet Mission Plan (members: Pethick-Lawrence, Stafford Cripps, A.V. Alexander); came into being 1946.
  • Total 389 members (296 British India + 93 Princely States); partly elected (indirectly), partly nominated.
  • First meeting 9 Dec 1946; Muslim League boycotted; after Partition, 299 seats. Temporary chairman Sachchidananda Sinha; permanent President Rajendra Prasad; Constitutional Advisor B.N. Rau.
  • As a legislative body it was headed by G.V. Mavalankar. National Flag adopted 22 July 1947; Anthem & Song adopted 24 Jan 1950.
Key committees of the Constituent Assembly
CommitteeChairman
Union Powers Committee / Union Constitution CommitteeJawaharlal Nehru

PREAMBLE — DECODED
Source: "We, the People of India". Nature: Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic, Republic.
Objectives: Justice (social, economic, political), Liberty (thought, expression, belief, faith, worship), Equality (status & opportunity), Fraternity.
Borrowed: Liberty-Equality-Fraternity → French Revolution; ideals of Justice → Russian Revolution.
Dates: Constitution adopted 26 Nov 1949 (Constitution Day); came into force 26 Jan 1950. Vallabhbhai Patel = "Bismarck of India".

BHARAT MATA & THE CONSTITUTION'S ART
  • In the painting, Bharat Mata has four hands holding: a book, cloth, paddy/crops, and a garland (mala).
  • Abanindranath Tagore also founded the Indian Society of Oriental Art.
  • Nandalal Bose (a student there) decorated/illustrated the Constitution.

BENGAL PARTITION & SWADESHI (1905)
Announced under Viceroy Curzon (July 1905), enforced 16 Oct 1905. Real aim: weaken Bengal (the nerve-centre of nationalism) on Hindu–Muslim lines; stated reason: "administrative".
1906 Calcutta Congress (President Dadabhai Naoroji) adopted the Swaraj, Boycott, National Education & Swadeshi resolutions. V.O. Chidambaram Pillai founded the Swadeshi Steam Navigation Company.
PARTITION ANNULLED & THE THREE DELHI DURBARS
Partition of Bengal was annulled in 1911 at the Third Delhi Durbar (Viceroy Hardinge II); capital shifted from Calcutta to Delhi (announced 1911, shifted 1912).
Durbars: 1877 (Lytton — Vernacular Press Act & Arms Act, 1878) · 1903 (Curzon) · 1911 (Hardinge II).

📿 Ancient History — The Sangam Age

SANGAM AGE — ESSENTIALS
  • Period: 3rd century BC – 3rd century AD. Three Sangams: 1st & 3rd at Madurai, 2nd at Kapatapuram.
  • Texts split into narrative (Melkanakku — Ettuthokai, Pattuppattu) and didactic (Kilkanakku). Kilkanakku divides into Aham (love) & Puram (war).
  • Silappadikaram — by Ilango Adigal (Kovalan–Madhavi–Kannagi; Kannagi worshipped as goddess of chastity / the Pattini cult, started by Chera king Senguttuvan).
  • Manimekalai — epic by Sattanar.

💡 Exam Tips from This Class
  • Use the +89 formula to convert a Union Article to its State equivalent (e.g., Art 77 → Art 166).
  • For "theory → scientist" questions, don't confuse plate tectonics (McKenzie & Parker / Morgan) with the convection current idea (Arthur Holmes).
  • Money supply: M1 = Narrow, M3 = Broad — the most-asked pair.
  • Watch the state mapping of national parks & nuclear plants (Kuno→MP, Dudhwa→UP, BARC→Maharashtra).
  • Sangam emblems: Chera=Bow & Arrow, Pandya=Fish, Chola=Tiger.
  • "Father of" tags: Minto → Communal Electorate; Ambedkar → the Constitution; Patel → "Bismarck of India".
