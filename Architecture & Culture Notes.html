⑤ Nagara vs Dravidian — Statements 🛕
Q5. Statements on structural differences between Nagara & Dravidian styles. (S1: Dravidian temples are enclosed within a compound wall, absent in Nagara. S2: The central tower shape in the Dravidian style is similar to the curving shikhara of Nagara.)
A
Only Statement 1 is correct ✔
✅ Answer: Only S1 correct. S2 wrong — Dravidian central tower = Vimana (pyramidal), NOT a curving shikhara
  • Garbhagriha = sanctum sanctorum (deity's idol installed)
  • Mandapa = pillared hall: Ardha Mandapa, Mandapa, Maha Mandapa
  • Shikhara (Nagara, curvy) → on top: Amalaka (gooseberry-shaped) → Kalasha
  • Vimana = Dravidian main tower; Gopuram = tall entrance gateway (South India)
  • Vallabhi = rectangular type; Jagati = raised platform
  • Nagara = North Indian style; Dravidian = South Indian style
