⚛ Newton's Laws of Motion ⚛

Definition: Three fundamental laws given by Sir Isaac Newton (1687) that describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting on it.


  • Force (F) → push or pull. Unit: newton (N)
  • Mass (m) → quantity of matter (kg)
  • Acceleration (a) → rate of change of velocity (m/s²)

↑ Force applied on a block produces acceleration

💡 Trick: FMA → "Force Makes Acceleration"

1st Law → Law of Inertia 🛑

"A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless an external force acts on it."

If Fnet = 0 ⇒ a = 0

  • Inertia of rest – passengers jerk backwards when bus starts
  • Inertia of motion – we fall forward when bus stops suddenly
  • Inertia ∝ mass (heavier → more inertia)

↑ Daily-life example of inertia of motion

⚠ Important: No force needed to KEEP moving, only to CHANGE motion!

2nd Law → F = ma 💪

"Rate of change of momentum is directly proportional to applied force."

p = m × v F = dp/dt F = m × a 1 N = 1 kg·m/s²

⚠ Same force → small mass = MORE acceleration!

↑ Same force F, different mass → different acceleration

💡 Trick: Double the mass → half the acceleration (same F)

✦ t.me/Exam_Zen ✦

3rd Law → Action = Reaction 🔁

"To every action there is an equal and opposite reaction."

FAB = − FBA

  • Forces act on different bodies → never cancel!
  • Rocket propulsion 🚀
  • Walking – we push ground backward, ground pushes us forward
  • Gun recoil after firing

↑ Rocket: gases pushed down, rocket moves up

⚠ Remember: Action & reaction act on DIFFERENT bodies, so they never cancel!

Important Derived Formulas 📐

  • Weight: W = m × g (g = 9.8 m/s²)
  • Friction: f = μ × N
  • Impulse: J = F × Δt = Δp
  • Momentum conservation: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
💡 Impulse explains why cricketers pull hands back while catching!
