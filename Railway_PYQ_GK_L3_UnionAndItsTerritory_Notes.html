① Part I & Article 1 — Union of States 🏛️
Q1. In which Article of the Constitution is it written that "India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States"?
A
Article 1 ✔
B
Article 2
C
Article 3
D
Article 4
✅ Answer: A — Article 1: "India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States" (Part I, Arts 1–4)

Why "Union of States" & not "Federation"? (Ambedkar's logic):

  • States did not form India by an agreement among themselves
  • So states have no right to secede from the Union
  • India = indestructible Union of destructible states

USA contrast: USA = destructible Union of indestructible states (states can't be altered, but the union was formed by agreement). India is the opposite.

③ Articles 2, 3, 4 — New States & Territory 🗺️
Q3. Admission and establishment of new states is mentioned in which Article of the Constitution?
A
Article 2 ✔
B
Article 3
C
Article 1
D
Article 4
✅ Answer: A — Art 2 = admission/establishment of new states (bringing foreign territory into India). Art 3 = formation/alteration of existing states.
ArticleProvision
2Admission / establishment of new states (foreign territory → India)
3Formation of new states; alter area/boundary/name; split or merge existing states
4Changes under Art 2 & 3 are NOT deemed amendments under Art 368 (done by simple majority)

Berubari Union Case (1960): To cede Indian territory to another country, a Constitutional Amendment (Art 368) IS required → 9th Amendment (Nehru–Noon Agreement, to Pakistan) & 100th Amendment (enclave exchange with Bangladesh). Taking foreign territory in = only Art 2 (simple majority).

Q4. Which of the following provisions is covered under Article 4 of the Constitution?
A
Fundamental Rights
B
Emergency Provisions
C
Laws for Reorganization of States ✔
D
Financial Powers
✅ Answer: C — Art 4 = laws for reorganization of states (changes under Art 2 & 3 are not treated as constitutional amendments)
CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT (Art 368, Part XX)
2 types: (1) Special majority · (2) Special majority + ratification by ≥ half the states
Simple-majority changes (Art 2 & 3) are NOT Art 368 amendments
🧠 42nd Amdt added 3 words to Preamble = "ISS" → Socialist, Secular, Integrity (NOT Sovereign)

B

C

✅ Answer: A — Andhra Pradesh = first linguistic state (1953), after Potti Sriramulu's fast-unto-death

Commissions on States Reorganization:
